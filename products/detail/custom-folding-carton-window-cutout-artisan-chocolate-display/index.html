Production Workflow

Every artisan chocolate carton follows our validated 9-stage production workflow, designed to maintain food safety and print quality:

  1. Board Selection & Incoming QC — 350gsm CCNB (standard) or SBS/FBB sourced from FSC-certified mills. Incoming inspection checks caliper (±5%), moisture content (6-8%), and surface smoothness (Bekk >80s).

  2. Prepress & Plate Making — CTP (Computer-to-Plate) thermal plate imaging at 2,400 DPI. G7 gray balance calibration. Press fingerprint characterization for color consistency.

  3. KBA 1050 4-Color Offset Printing — CMYK process with optional Pantone spot colors. 175 LPI screen ruling. Inline densitometer for real-time color monitoring. ΔE <2.0 maintained across the full run.

  4. Matte Lamination — 25μm matte BOPP film applied via hot-roll lamination at 120°C with 2.5 MPa pressure. Provides scuff resistance, moisture barrier, and premium soft-touch feel.

  5. Die-Cutting & Window Creation — Custom steel-rule die board with precision laser-cut window aperture. Flatbed die-cutting on Bobst SP 102 with ±0.15mm tolerance. 100% window aperture inspection.

  6. PET Window Film Insertion — 0.3mm crystal-clear PET film (or biodegradable PLA alternative) heat-sealed behind die-cut window. Haze <3% per ASTM D1003 verified per batch. Food-contact migration tested per EU 10/2011.

  7. Folding & Gluing — Hot-melt EVA adhesive applied on automatic folder-gluer at 160-180°C. Crease lines pre-broken for clean folding. Auto-lock bottom mechanism tested for secure engagement.

  8. Quality Inspection — AQL 2.5 Level II sampling. Window clarity, glue bond strength (≥8N/cm²), print registration (±0.5mm), fold endurance (100+ cycles), color consistency (ΔE <2.0).

  9. Flat-Packing & Shipping — Cartons ship flat (knocked down) for maximum transport efficiency. Packed in corrugated master cases with desiccant packs. Fumigation-free palletization. 3,000-8,000 cartons per pallet depending on size.

Sustainability

Bincai folding cartons are designed for recyclability without compromising food safety or shelf impact:

  • FSC-Certified Paperboard — All paperboard sourced from FSC-certified forests and controlled sources (FSC-C147399). Your packaging can carry the FSC Mix label, demonstrating commitment to responsible forestry.

  • Curbside Recyclable — Paperboard cartons with PET windows under 30% of total surface area are accepted in most municipal recycling streams. We can provide repulpability test data for your specific window-to-board ratio upon request.

  • PLA Window Option — For brands targeting composting, we offer PLA (polylactic acid) window film derived from corn starch. PLA is certified industrially compostable per EN 13432. Note: PLA requires industrial composting facilities (58°C+) — verify with your local waste infrastructure.

  • Water-Based Inks & Coatings — Our aqueous coatings and water-based flexo inks emit minimal VOCs during printing and do not interfere with paper recyclability. Soy-based offset inks available for brands seeking maximum eco-credentials.

  • Flat-Ship Efficiency — Knocked-down shipping reduces transport carbon footprint by 70-85% compared to pre-assembled cartons. Fewer trucks, less fuel, lower cost.

  • Closed-Loop Trim Recycling — All paper offcuts from die-cutting and guillotining are baled and returned to paper mills. Printing plates, ink containers, and corrugated packaging waste are segregated and processed through certified recycling partners.

FAQ

Q: Is the PET window food-safe for direct chocolate contact?

A: The PET window film is food-contact certified per FDA 21 CFR 177.1630 and EU Regulation 10/2011 with overall migration limit <10 mg/dm². The film is inserted behind the window cutout and typically does not make direct contact with unwrapped chocolates if an insert tray is used. For direct-contact configurations (chocolates placed directly against the film), we provide additional migration testing documentation and can upgrade to a food-grade barrier film. Note: PET is not a barrier to oxygen — for long-shelf-life chocolate (6+ months), we recommend additional barrier packaging inside the carton.

Q: Can you produce custom window shapes — hearts, stars, company logos?

A: Absolutely. Our in-house die-making workshop uses CAD + CNC laser cutting to produce custom steel-rule dies with any window shape — hearts, stars, botanical outlines, brand logos, geometric patterns. Die production takes 2-3 days. The only constraint is that the window must be fully enclosed (no open edges) and at least 15mm from carton edges to maintain structural integrity. Complex shapes with fine details (<2mm line width) may require design adjustment for manufacturability.

Q: What insert tray options work best with chocolate cartons?

A: We offer three insert tray solutions: (1) Die-cut cardboard tray — most economical, custom cavities for each chocolate piece, can be printed to match the carton. (2) Clear PET thermoform tray — showcases each chocolate like jewelry, food-safe, snap-fit into carton. (3) Molded pulp tray — eco-friendly, compostable, textured kraft finish, great for bean-to-bar and craft chocolate brands. All trays are custom-designed to your chocolate dimensions with 0.5mm clearance per cavity.

Q: How does the auto-lock bottom compare to a tuck-top closure — which is better for chocolate?

A: Auto-lock bottom (crash-lock) is strongly recommended for chocolate packaging for three reasons: (1) It locks mechanically without adhesive, eliminating any risk of glue odor transferring to chocolate. (2) The flat bottom provides a stable base — chocolates won’t tip or shift during retail display. (3) Assembly speed is 3-4x faster than manual tuck-bottom folding, critical for production lines filling thousands of boxes. The trade-off is slightly higher die cost and minimum board thickness of 300gsm for reliable locking.

Q: What’s the minimum order for a custom chocolate carton with a unique window shape?

A: MOQ is 1,000 units. The custom die board for your window shape is a one-time tooling cost of $80-150 (credited back on orders over 5,000 units). Die boards last 50,000-100,000 impressions before re-sharpening. We recommend ordering 1,000-3,000 units for your first run to test market response and refine the design — our per-unit cost at 1,000 units is approximately $0.45-0.65 including custom die, printing, lamination, window film, and gluing.

Q: Can the carton withstand chocolate bloom from temperature fluctuations during shipping?

A: Chocolate bloom (fat or sugar bloom) occurs when chocolate experiences temperature fluctuations causing cocoa butter to migrate to the surface. While the carton itself doesn’t prevent bloom, we offer two protective measures: (1) Aluminum-metallized inner liner (bonded to the interior board surface) that provides a partial moisture and oxygen barrier, reducing bloom risk. (2) Insulated master case configuration with reflective bubble wrap for temperature-sensitive shipping lanes. For summer shipping to warm climates, we strongly recommend gel ice packs and expedited cold-chain logistics, which our export team can coordinate.

Q: Will the carton look premium enough for a $40 box of artisan truffles?

A: Yes — and here’s why. The combination of matte lamination (soft-touch, fingerprint-resistant), a crystal-clear PET window that makes the product the hero, and the structural precision of auto-lock bottom construction creates a premium unboxing experience. For ultra-premium positioning ($40+ per box), we recommend adding: soft-touch velvet lamination, gold hot foil stamping on the brand logo, a ribbon pull tab, and a custom thermoform tray that cradles each chocolate individually. These upgrades increase per-unit cost by $0.15-0.40 and elevate the perceived value significantly. Many of our artisan chocolate clients report customers keeping the box as a keepsake — the ultimate packaging ROI.
