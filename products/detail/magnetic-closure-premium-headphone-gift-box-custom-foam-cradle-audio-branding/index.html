FAQ

Q: How do you design the foam cradle to fit our specific headphone model accurately?

A: Our foam design process: (1) You send us one sample headphone unit (or provide detailed 3D CAD/STL files), (2) Our engineering team creates a 2D tracing or 3D scan of the headphone profile, (3) We design the cavity layout in CAD with 0.5mm clearance for insertion/removal ease, (4) CNC-machined foam sample produced for your approval (5-7 days), (5) Upon approval, a production foam mold is created for mass production. The entire sampling cycle is 7-10 days. For subsequent reorders, the foam mold is stored and reused — no additional tooling charge.

Q: Can the box be designed to ship directly to consumers — does it survive courier handling?

A: Yes, our headphone gift boxes are engineered for both retail presentation and e-commerce shipping. The 2.5mm rigid greyboard construction with full-coverage foam cradle passes ISTA 1A drop testing (76cm free-fall, 10 drops). For brands shipping directly to consumers, we recommend adding an outer corrugated shipping sleeve — we can produce both the gift box and the shipping sleeve as a matched set. The gift box itself can also serve as the primary shipping container for lighter headphones (<500g) with our heavy-duty board upgrade (3.0mm caliper).

Q: What’s the difference between EVA and PU foam for the cradle — which should I choose?

A: EVA (ethylene-vinyl acetate) foam is firmer, more durable, and provides excellent shock absorption — ideal for heavier over-ear headphones and products that will be shipped frequently. EVA holds its shape permanently and resists compression set. PU (polyurethane) foam is softer with a more luxurious hand-feel — preferred for premium unboxing experiences and lighter in-ear monitors. PU foam is available in open-cell (breathable, softer) and closed-cell (firmer, moisture-resistant) variants. For most consumer audio products, EVA with a fabric-laminated surface offers the best balance of protection, presentation, and longevity.
