Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the difference between B-flute and E-flute for subscription boxes? A: B-flute (3mm) offers better crush resistance for heavier items (2-10 kg) and is the industry standard for subscription boxes. Its thicker profile provides better stacking strength in warehouse and transit. E-flute (1.5mm) has a smoother, flatter surface for higher print quality and a more refined unboxing feel — best suited for cosmetics, jewelry, and lightweight subscription kits under 2 kg. We recommend B-flute for most applications; E-flute when brand presentation and print quality are the priority.

Q: Can I print inside the box for branded unboxing experience? A: Yes. Interior flexo printing on the inside liner is available for branded unboxing messages, social media handles, care instructions, or “thank you” notes. For higher print quality, we can also do offset litho-lamination on the interior liner. This is a popular feature for DTC subscription brands wanting to create a memorable unboxing moment without additional printed inserts.

Q: How does the tear strip work and is it reliable? A: The tear strip is a reinforced PP filament tape applied inline during the folder-gluer process. It’s embedded between the outer and inner liners with a pull tab extending 15-20mm. Independently tested for 500+ pulls without failure — the tear follows a clean, straight edge and leaves no sharp burrs. The strip can be positioned on the top panel, side panel, or wrapping around three sides for a full-open unboxing experience.

Q: What is the minimum order for custom full-color flexo print? A: MOQ is 500-1,000 units depending on box size and complexity. Flexo printing plates cost $80-200 per color (one-time, reusable for repeat orders). For digital print (no plates), MOQ is 100 units. For offset litho-lamination (premium print quality), MOQ is 3,000 units. We recommend flexo for subscription box volumes of 1,000+ where per-unit cost efficiency matters.

Q: Can the self-seal strip handle returns and exchanges? A: Yes. The double-sided self-seal adhesive strip is designed for multiple open-close cycles (tested for 20+ cycles) — customers can reseal the box for returns without additional tape. The adhesive is pressure-sensitive and temperature-stable from -20°C to 60°C. For subscription brands with high return rates (apparel, shoes), we recommend upgrading to a reinforced adhesive formulation.

Q: Are your corrugated boxes compliant with Amazon and major retailer packaging requirements? A: Yes. Our corrugated boxes meet ISTA 3A (parcel delivery simulation), ISTA 6-Amazon.com (SIOC — Ships In Own Container), and Amazon Frustration-Free Packaging (FFP) standards. We can provide ISTA test reports and Amazon Packaging Certification on request. All boxes use water-based flexo inks that are low-VOC and compliant with EU Packaging Directive 94/62/EC. FSC certification documentation is provided with every shipment.

Q: What is the typical lead time for custom subscription boxes? A: Design and structural sampling: 3-5 working days. Flexo printing plate production: 3-5 working days (first order only). Mass production: 10-15 working days after sample and plate approval. Sea freight: 12-15 days to US West Coast, 22-28 days to Europe. Typical total timeline for a first order: 5-6 weeks. Repeat orders (plates already made): 3-4 weeks. We recommend planning subscription box launches 8 weeks ahead to allow for sampling, revisions, and shipping.
