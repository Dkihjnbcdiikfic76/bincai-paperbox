FAQ

Q: Will the tear strip damage the garment inside when the customer opens it?

A: No. The tear strip is positioned on the exterior surface of the box and tears through the outer liner and flute only. The inner surface remains intact. Additionally, the tear path is designed to run along the box’s top edge or side seam — away from where the garment sits. We’ve tested this design across thousands of shipments with delicate fabrics including silk, cashmere, and linen with zero garment damage. For extra protection, we can include a tissue paper insert that creates a physical barrier between the box interior and the garment.

Q: Which flute is best for shipping shirts?

A: For single t-shirts and lightweight apparel, E-flute (1.5mm) is our recommendation — it provides the best print surface for branded graphics while delivering adequate crush protection for non-fragile garments. The compact profile also keeps dimensional weight shipping costs low, which is critical for e-commerce margins. For multiple garments or heavier items like hoodies and sweaters, B-flute (2.5mm) offers additional crush resistance. C-flute (3.5mm) is available for maximum protection but is generally overkill for apparel unless shipping in bulk.

Q: Can I print my full brand design in multiple colors?

A: Yes, via two paths: (1) Water-based flexo printing supports 1-3 colors (typically your brand’s primary palette) at the most cost-effective price point — ideal for startups and mid-volume brands; (2) Litho-laminate printing provides full CMYK photo-quality graphics — identical quality to retail packaging — perfect for premium fashion brands where the unboxing experience is a key brand touchpoint. Litho-laminate costs approximately 30-40% more than flexo but delivers dramatically better print quality.

Q: Does the box hold up in standard e-commerce shipping conditions?

A: Yes. Our corrugated garment mailers pass ISTA 1A testing (10 drops from 760mm on all faces, edges, and corners) — the same standard used by major carriers. Single-wall B-flute provides adequate crush protection for folded garments in standard e-commerce shipping. For brands shipping high-value designer garments, we recommend upgrading to double-wall construction for maximum peace of mind.

Q: What’s the minimum order quantity and lead time?

A: MOQ is 500 units for standard sizes using our existing rotary die tooling. For custom-size boxes requiring new die fabrication, MOQ is 1,000 units. Free pre-production samples (flat structure and printed) are provided within 5–7 working days. Standard production lead time is 10–14 working days. Rush orders can be accommodated (7–10 working days) with a 15% surcharge. We recommend ordering samples first to validate the box dimensions against your folded garments.
