Why Reverse-Tuck?

The reverse-tuck style is the workhorse of the folding carton world for good reason: it assembles lightning-fast (critical for operations packing thousands of bars per shift), ships flat (saving 70% on freight vs. rigid boxes), and the opposing tuck direction creates structural tension that keeps the carton securely closed without glue or magnets. For chocolate makers scaling from farmers’ market to national distribution, reverse-tuck cartons are the practical path from handmade to mass retail.

Food Safety Compliance

All materials in contact with chocolate are FDA 21 CFR compliant:

  • SBS board: FDA 21 CFR 176.170 (components of paper and paperboard in contact with aqueous and fatty foods)
  • PET window film: FDA 21 CFR 177.1630 (polyethylene phthalate polymers)
  • Hot-stamp foil: No direct food contact — applied only to exterior
  • Printing inks: Low-migration, low-odor UV-cured inks suitable for indirect food contact
  • Side-seam adhesive: FDA 21 CFR 175.105 compliant water-based PVA

Quality Control

  1. Board Caliper: Measured per pallet — within ±5% of spec
  2. Print Registration: CMYK within ±0.1mm, spot colors within ±0.15mm
  3. Foil Registration: Hot-stamp alignment within ±0.3mm relative to print
  4. Window Film Bond: Peel test — 2N minimum bond strength
  5. Carton Assembly Test: 100 random cartons assembled — zero assembly failures
  6. Food Contact Migration: Periodic third-party lab testing for ink migration
  7. Final AQL 1.5: Visual — print, foil, window clarity, carton integrity
