Seven-Stage QC Gates

  1. Incoming Material Inspection — greyboard caliper and moisture content (6–8%), art paper/leatherette grammage verification, N52 magnet pull-force certification, satin ribbon color and width verification
  2. Pre-Production Proofing — structural die alignment with CAD, foil die fit check, leatherette wrap color match to Pantone reference, velvet tray cavity fit test with sample product
  3. In-Process Printing & Wrapping — Heidelberg Prinect color verification every 500 sheets (ΔE ≤ 2.5), leatherette/paper wrap adhesion inspection, corner miter alignment ±0.2mm
  4. Foil Stamping & Finishing — foil registration ±0.15mm on Bobst SP 102 BMA, coating uniformity (gloss meter 70–85 GU at 60° for gloss, 3–8 GU for matte), embossing depth consistency
  5. Tray Assembly — CNC cavity dimensional verification ±0.3mm, velvet flocking coverage 100%, tray-to-box fit test, ribbon pull-tab installation and bar-tack check
  6. Closure & Function Testing — magnetic closure alignment gauge, lid open/close smoothness (no hinge binding), tray removal/insertion force test, ribbon pull function
  7. Pre-Shipment Inspection — AQL 2.5 Level II per ISO 2859-1, ISTA 1A drop test with simulated product weight, 100% visual inspection under D65 lighting, final lid alignment and closure verification

FAQ

Q: What’s the difference between leatherette and genuine leather wrap?

A: Leatherette (PU) is our standard premium surface — it offers excellent color consistency, scuff resistance, and a $1.50–3.00/unit price point. Genuine leather wrap is available for ultra-premium editions at $5.00–8.00/unit and offers natural grain variation, patina development over time, and the unmistakable leather aroma. For most corporate gifting programs, leatherette delivers the right balance of luxury perception and cost-efficiency.

Q: Can the velvet tray be designed for products I haven’t finalized yet?

A: Yes. Our structural design team works from CAD drawings, 3D models (STEP/IGES), or physical product samples to CNC-mill a tray that exactly matches your product dimensions. We can also design the tray with 0.5mm clearance for easy product removal — standard for executive gifts where the recipient will frequently access the contents.

Q: What’s the minimum order for a fully custom tray layout?

A: MOQ is 500 units for a custom CNC-milled tray — the same as the box itself. There is no separate tray tooling charge. For trial orders, we can produce 50–100 samples with your custom tray at a slightly higher unit cost before committing to full production.

Q: How do you ensure the box looks identical across multiple production runs (e.g., annual award ceremonies)?

A: We maintain a master reference sample and detailed production specification sheet for every client. Our Heidelberg Prinect color management system archives your specific color profile, and our material library retains your exact leatherette lot reference. For repeat orders, we pull the archived master sample and match to it — not to a new standard — ensuring year-over-year consistency. ΔE ≤ 2.5 is maintained between production runs separated by 12+ months.

Q: Can the box include both a velvet tray and a document/certificate sleeve?

A: Yes — this is one of our most popular configurations for award ceremonies. The standard layout has the velvet tray in the base and a satin-lined document sleeve mounted under the lid, holding an A4 certificate (tri-folded) or A5 booklet. For multi-item presentations, we can design a two-tier configuration with the velvet tray on top and a document compartment beneath.

Q: Is the magnetic closure strong enough for international shipping?

A: Absolutely. Our 6× N52 neodymium magnet configuration provides ≥30N total closure force — sufficient to keep the lid securely closed through ISTA 1A drop testing (10 drops from 760mm) with a 2kg product load. For extra-heavy contents or long-haul air/sea freight, we can upgrade to 8× magnets or add a ribbon-tie as a secondary closure. The box also passes ISTA 3A parcel delivery simulation for e-commerce fulfillment.
