FAQ

Q: What’s the minimum order quantity for custom-shaped boxes? A: MOQ starts at 500 units for custom die-cut shapes. The steel rule die is a one-time tooling cost ($100–$400 depending on complexity) and can be reused for reorders. For standard rectangular shapes without custom dies, MOQ can be as low as 300 units.

Q: How long does custom die creation take? A: Steel rule die fabrication takes 3–5 working days after dieline approval. We send the CAD dieline for confirmation within 24 hours of order placement. Once the die is ready, production takes 7–15 working days depending on quantity and finishing complexity.

Q: Can you match an existing box shape from a sample? A: Yes — we can reverse-engineer any paper box from a physical sample or detailed measurements. Send us your sample via courier and our engineering team will create the dieline, material specifications, and a production sample within 5–7 working days for your approval.

Q: What material thickness options are available for rigid special-shape boxes? A: Greyboard thickness ranges from 800gsm (lightweight, ~1mm) to 2,500gsm (heavy luxury, ~3mm). Most rigid gift boxes use 1,200–1,600gsm for the optimal balance of weight and structural integrity. For very small boxes (jewelry), 800–1,000gsm is sufficient; for large format boxes, 1,600–2,000gsm ensures rigidity.
