Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the difference between soft-touch matte lamination and standard matte? A: Standard matte lamination applies a textured film that reduces glare but can feel slightly plastic. Bincai’s soft-touch matte uses a proprietary 3–5μm velvet coating that feels like fine suede — it’s the “unboxing moment” finish that luxury brands demand. Soft-touch resists fingerprints significantly better than standard matte and doesn’t show handling wear after repeated customer interaction. It’s available in clear (over any printed color) or tinted for a colored velvet effect.

Q: Can spot UV be applied to specific design elements like a logo or pattern? A: Yes — this is exactly where spot UV shines. We create a UV mask plate from your artwork that applies high-gloss coating only to designated areas: logo, brand name, decorative patterns, borders, or product imagery. The result is a striking contrast — matte background with glossy elements that catch light and draw the eye. On Bincai’s Heidelberg 7+1 UV press, the spot UV mask is registered to the print with ±0.2mm precision, so even fine typography and thin lines get crisp gloss treatment without bleeding into the matte area.

Q: What interior options work best with matte+spot UV rigid boxes? A: The contrast exterior pairs beautifully with these interior treatments: velvet flocking (black or dark grey creates dramatic reveal), satin-lined base with die-cut foam insert (premium product cradling), magnetic flap with hidden ribbon pull (seamless exterior, elegant opening), or a printed inner lid with brand story and gold foil accents. For maximum impact, we recommend a dark velvet interior — the transition from sophisticated matte exterior to plush dark interior creates the strongest unboxing contrast. Free interior mockup renderings provided with your quote.

Q: How durable is the matte lamination over time and during shipping? A: Bincai’s matte lamination undergoes rigorous durability testing: cross-hatch adhesion test (ISO 2409) with 100% pass rate, Taber abrasion test (500+ cycles without visible wear), temperature cycling (-20°C to +60°C, 72 hours, zero delamination), and humidity exposure (90% RH, 48 hours, zero bubbling or peeling). For shipping, our boxes pass ISTA 1A drop testing (10 drops from 760mm) without lamination damage. This durability means your packaging arrives at retail looking as pristine as it left our factory.

Q: What MOQ and lead times apply for custom matte+spot UV rigid boxes? A: Standard MOQ is 500 units per design, covering custom dimensions, CMYK+PMS printing, matte lamination, and spot UV mask. For initial sampling and photo shoots, we can produce 200 units at a premium. Lead time is 15–20 working days from artwork approval (5–7 days for pre-production samples). Repeat orders drop to 10–12 working days. For rush orders (7–10 days), a 15% surcharge applies. Volume pricing tiers begin at 2,500, 5,000, 10,000, and 25,000+ units — contact our team for a detailed quotation within 24 hours.
