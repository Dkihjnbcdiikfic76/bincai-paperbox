FAQ

Q: How durable is the hot foil stamping — will it scratch off during retail handling or shipping?

A: Our hot foil stamping is engineered for durability, not just aesthetics. We use the Bobst SP 102 BMA autoplaten at precisely 200°C ±5°C with controlled dwell time and pressure — the optimal parameters for maximum foil-to-substrate bond strength. Every production run undergoes ASTM D3359 cross-cut tape testing and must achieve a 5B rating (zero foil removal) before shipment. Additionally, our 3M #810 tape peel test simulates retail handling abrasion. For products requiring extra durability (frequent handling, high-humidity environments), we recommend a post-foil aqueous over-coat or soft-touch lamination applied after stamping — this seals the foil edge and prevents edge-lift while preserving the metallic brilliance.

Q: Can you combine multiple foil colors on the same box?

A: Yes — we offer multi-foil registration with up to 3 different foil colors on a single box. Each foil color requires a separate stamping die and pass through the Bobst autoplaten, with precision registration maintained at ±0.15mm between foil colors. Popular combinations include: gold logo + silver accent lines, rose gold brand name + copper decorative border, and holographic foil background + matte gold text overlay. Multi-foil adds approximately 15–25% per additional foil color and requires a minimum order of 3,000 units to amortize the additional die and setup costs.

Q: What’s the difference between soft-touch, matte lamination, and aqueous coating — and which is right for my cosmetic brand?

A: These three finishes serve different brand aesthetics and functional needs: Soft-touch lamination creates a velvety, suede-like tactile sensation that screams luxury — it’s the most popular choice for premium skincare and fragrance cartons because it makes the box irresistible to touch on-shelf. It also resists fingerprints, which is critical for dark-colored boxes. Trade-off: it’s the most expensive option (+20–30% over matte lamination). Matte lamination delivers a smooth, non-reflective surface that reads as sophisticated and understated — ideal for clean beauty, natural cosmetics, and minimalist brands. It’s more affordable (+10–15%) and provides good scuff resistance. Aqueous coating is a water-based varnish applied inline on-press — it’s the most economical option (no extra cost for basic gloss), fully repulpable/recyclable, and suitable for brands prioritizing end-of-life sustainability. It can be gloss, matte, or satin. Many brands combine aqueous matte coating with spot UV on the logo — achieving a premium look at moderate cost while maintaining full recyclability.

Finish Selector — Soft-Touch vs Matte Lamination vs Aqueous Coating

The finish you choose defines how your cosmetic carton feels in a customer’s hand — and influences purchase decision. Here’s how to pick:

Your Brand / ProductBest FinishWhy
Premium skincare ($60–150), anti-aging, luxury serumsSoft-Touch LaminationVelvety feel = luxury signal; fingerprint-resistant on dark boxes; +20–30% cost
Clean beauty, natural cosmetics, minimalist brandsMatte LaminationUnderstated sophistication; smooth non-reflective surface; +10–15% cost
Mass-market cosmetics, drugstore brands, value setsAqueous Coating (matte or gloss)Most economical; fully recyclable (no plastic film); inline application
Fragrance gift sets, limited editionsSoft-Touch + Spot UV on logoDual texture — velvety body + glossy logo pop; memorable tactile experience
Eco-positioned brand, “zero plastic” claimAqueous Matte + DebossNo plastic lamination; deboss adds tactile interest without coating; fully repulpable
Dark-colored boxes (black, navy, burgundy)Soft-TouchHides fingerprints that plague dark matte boxes; looks pristine longer on-shelf
Bright white boxes, high-gloss photographyGloss LaminationAmplifies color vibrancy; makes product photography “pop”; reflects retail lighting

The fingerprint test: Take your box mockup to a retail environment. Touch it 20 times — the way customers will. If fingerprints are visible, you need soft-touch or a lighter color. Dark matte boxes without soft-touch look worn within hours on a retail shelf. This is the #1 finish mistake we see new cosmetic brands make — choosing matte lamination on a black box because it looks elegant in a 3D render, then discovering the first customer who picks it up leaves a visible fingerprint that the next 50 customers see.
