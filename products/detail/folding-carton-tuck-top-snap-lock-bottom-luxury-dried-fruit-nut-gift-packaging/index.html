Guangdong Bincai Color Printing Co., Ltd. (founded 2003) delivers premium folding carton packaging engineered for luxury dried fruits, gourmet nuts, and upscale confectionery gifting. Featuring a tuck-top closure paired with a snap-lock (auto-lock) bottom, this carton ships flat to minimize logistics costs and assembles in seconds at the point of filling — no glue, no tape, no equipment required.

Manufactured in our 18,000 sqm dual-factory in Foshan, Guangdong, the carton body uses 300–400gsm coated duplex board (CCNB or FBB) printed with vibrant CMYK plus hot foil stamping (gold, silver, rose gold, or holographic) on our KBA Rapida 105 4-color offset press and Bobst hot foil stamper. The snap-lock bottom mechanism is precision die-cut with reinforced crease lines for reliable assembly across thousands of units. Food-grade soy-based inks and water-based PVA adhesives ensure compliance with FDA 21 CFR 175.105 for indirect food contact.

FAQ

Q: Is the snap-lock bottom reliable for heavier products like nuts and dried fruits?

A: Yes. Our snap-lock bottom design is engineered with reinforced crease lines and locking tab geometry that has been tested to hold 1.5kg of product without bottom failure. The mechanism uses interlocking flaps that distribute weight across the entire bottom panel. For products exceeding 1kg, we recommend upgrading from 300gsm to 400gsm board for additional structural integrity. We provide filled-box testing with your actual product weight before full production.

Q: Can the hot foil stamping be combined with matte lamination?

A: Absolutely — and it’s a popular combination for premium food packaging. We apply matte lamination first (via thermal lamination), then hot foil stamp over the laminated surface. The contrast between the soft matte background and the reflective metallic foil creates exceptional shelf presence. We recommend matte lamination + gold foil for warm-toned products (dried fruits, nuts) and matte + silver foil for cooler-toned products (mints, white chocolate).

Q: Is the packaging food-safe for direct contact with dried fruits and nuts?

A: The box interior surface uses FDA 21 CFR 175.105 compliant water-based adhesive, and all inks are soy-based with VOC ≤0.5%. However, for direct food contact without an inner liner, we recommend products that are individually wrapped or naturally dry (nuts in shell, whole dried fruits). For unwrapped, oil-rich products like chocolate truffles, we recommend adding a food-grade inner bag or grease-resistant tissue liner — which we can supply as an integrated packaging solution.

Q: What’s the difference between CCNB, FBB, and SBS board options?

A: CCNB (Clay Coated News Back) is the most economical — grey back with white coated front, ideal for standard retail. FBB (Folding Box Board) has a cream/white back with brighter white front — better print quality and a more premium unboxing feel when opened. SBS (Solid Bleached Sulfate) is pure white throughout — the premium choice for luxury gifting. For dried fruit and nut gift boxes, we typically recommend FBB as the best value/premium balance, with SBS for ultra-luxury holiday collections.

Q: What’s the minimum order quantity and can I get printed samples?

A: MOQ is 500 units for standard sizes using our existing tooling. For custom sizes requiring new cutting die, MOQ is 1,000 units. For hot foil stamping, a custom foil die is required (one-time tooling fee of approximately $80–120 depending on size and complexity). Free pre-production samples (printed with your artwork) are provided within 5–7 working days. We strongly recommend ordering both blank structure samples and printed samples to validate the snap-lock function and foil quality before full production.
