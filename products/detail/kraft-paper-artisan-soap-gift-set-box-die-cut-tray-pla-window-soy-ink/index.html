Production Workflow

  1. Board Selection: FSC recycled kraft rolls received, grammage and moisture tested at incoming QC station
  2. Sheet Cutting: Precision sheeting to press format (typically 720×1020mm or 520×740mm) on Polar 137XT guillotine
  3. Printing: Soy-based CMYK offset on KBA 1050 4-color — single-pass printing with inline aqueous coating for scuff resistance
  4. Window Patching: PLA film applied by Bobst Ambition window patcher — ±0.5mm window-to-graphic registration. Water-based cold glue, no heat (preserves PLA optical clarity)
  5. Die-Cutting: Bobst Novacut 106-E autoplaten — ±0.15mm dimensional tolerance. Creasing rules calibrated to kraft board grain direction to prevent cracking
  6. Tray Die-Cutting: Separate die for internal tray — contoured cavities match soap bar geometry exactly
  7. Folding & Gluing: Bobst Masterfold 110 — hot-melt-free, water-based PVA only. Side-seam glue with 3-point verification
  8. Assembly & QC: Tray insertion, window inspection, AQL 2.5 final sampling audit before polybag and carton packing

FAQ

Q: Is the PLA window truly compostable, or does it require special facilities? A: The standard PLA film is EN 13432 certified for industrial composting — it requires 58°C+ temperatures and active microbial environment found in commercial facilities, not backyard compost. For home compostability (TÜV OK Compost HOME certified), we offer a specialty PLA grade at 10,000+ MOQ. Both options are bio-based and certified compostable.

Q: Can the soy inks handle moisture from fresh soap bars? A: Yes. Our soy-based inks are formulated with aqueous overprint coating that provides moisture resistance. For soap bars with high glycerin content (melt-and-pour bases), we recommend an additional matte aqueous coating to prevent any potential ink transfer. In 23 years of soap packaging production, we’ve had zero ink transfer complaints with this system.

Q: What’s the minimum order for custom tray cavity shapes? A: 500 units per cavity design. New die fabrication for custom tray contours costs $80-120 and takes 2-3 days. For irregular soap shapes, provide a physical sample or CAD file — we 3D-scan the bar and CNC-machine the cutting die to match.

Q: Can the kraft board be printed in white or metallic inks? A: White ink on kraft is possible but opacity is limited on the absorbent kraft surface — we typically recommend a white flood coat first (adds ~$0.08/unit). Metallic gold/silver soy-based inks are available and produce a warm, organic metallic effect unique to kraft — different from hot foil but very on-brand for natural products.

Q: How does Bincai verify the EN 13432 compliance of the PLA film annually? A: We require annual third-party test reports from our PLA film supplier (accredited lab, ISO 17025). We also conduct random disintegration verification using our in-house accelerated composting chamber (58°C, 90% RH, 12-week cycle per EN 13432 Annex A). Certificate of compliance is included with every shipment.
