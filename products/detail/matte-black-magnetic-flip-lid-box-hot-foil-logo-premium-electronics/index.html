FAQ

Q: Will the matte black surface show fingerprints and scratches like cheap matte boxes?

A: No. Our matte black uses either soft-touch lamination (velvet-tactile, oleophobic — actively resists fingerprints) or premium matte lamination with anti-scuff additive. We test both finishes for fingerprint visibility: soft-touch shows virtually zero fingerprints after handling; matte lamination shows minimal prints that wipe clean easily. Scuff resistance is tested per ISO 105-X12 with >4.0 grey scale rating (excellent). This is the same lamination technology used by flagship smartphone packaging — not the economy matte that scuffs white at the corners.

Q: Can the foam insert hold multiple items — device + cable + charger + manual?

A: Yes. We engineer multi-cavity EVA foam trays using your complete product BOM (bill of materials). A typical layout includes: primary device cavity (snug fit), cable channel (wrapped cable), charger/power brick cavity, accessory compartments (ear tips, adapters), and a finger-scoop or ribbon pull for easy manual removal. We optimize cavity layout to minimize foam waste while ensuring adequate wall thickness (minimum 5mm between cavities for structural integrity).

Q: What file format do you need for the device cavity design?

A: We accept STEP (.stp/.step), IGES (.igs), STL (3D mesh), or 2D engineering drawings with dimensions in millimeters. If you don’t have a 3D file, you can ship us a physical product sample and we’ll 3D-scan it to create the cavity geometry. For complex devices with buttons, ports, or irregular shapes, 3D CAD is strongly preferred for the most precise fit.

Q: Can you add wireless charging capability to the box itself?

A: Yes — as a value-add feature. We can engineer a slot in the foam tray to accommodate a Qi wireless charging coil and PCB, and add a cable passthrough for USB-C power. The box then serves as both packaging and a functional wireless charger — a popular premium feature for earbuds and smartwatch packaging. We don’t manufacture the electronics; you provide the charging module or we source it to your specification. This adds approximately $3–8 to unit cost depending on the charging module.

Q: What’s the difference between your boxes and the packaging used by major tech brands?

A: The materials and processes are identical — we use the same KBA and Heidelberg presses, same Bobst die-cutting and foil stamping, same matte/soft-touch lamination formulations, and same N52 magnets that produce flagship electronics packaging. The main differences are: (1) MOQ — we accept 500 units vs. 50,000+ from major-brand suppliers; (2) speed — 18–22 days vs. 45–60 days; (3) cost — our Foshan manufacturing economics are 30–50% lower than equivalent European or North American production. Many of our electronics brand customers launched on our packaging before scaling to major retail, and several continue using us for their premium SKUs while using local suppliers for mass-market lines.
