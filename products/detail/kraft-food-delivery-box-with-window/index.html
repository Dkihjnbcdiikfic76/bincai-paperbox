Product Overview

The Bincai Kraft Food Delivery Box with Window combines environmental responsibility with practical food presentation. Made from FSC-certified recycled kraft board (300-400gsm) with a clear window, this box lets customers see the food inside while maintaining structural integrity during delivery. Printed with soy-based inks on KBA 1050 4-color presses at Bincai’s 18,000 sqm integrated facility in Foshan, Guangdong. Ideal for bakeries, meal-prep services, salad bars, and sustainable food brands seeking compostable or recyclable takeaway packaging.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: PET window vs. PLA window — which should I choose and what are the trade-offs? A: PET windows offer superior clarity (90%+ light transmission), lower cost (~15% less than PLA), and are widely recyclable (Resin Code #1). The trade-off is that PET is petroleum-based and not biodegradable. PLA windows are plant-based (corn starch), industrially compostable (EN 13432 certified, disintegrates in 12 weeks), and align with zero-waste brand positioning. The trade-offs: PLA is slightly less transparent (85% light transmission), costs ~15% more, and has a lower heat deflection temperature — not recommended for hot food direct contact exceeding 50°C. For room-temperature foods (pastries, salads, sandwiches), PLA performs equivalently. For hot food delivery (60°C+), PET is recommended for window integrity.

Q: Can you print full-color branding on the kraft surface, or will the brown background affect color accuracy? A: Our KBA Rapida 105 4-color press prints directly on kraft with excellent results. The kraft brown background does shift color perception — whites appear cream, light colors take on warm undertones. This is actually a design feature that complements the eco-aesthetic. We recommend: (1) Use bold, saturated colors that pop against kraft (navy, forest green, burgundy, black); (2) Design with the kraft background as a color element rather than fighting it; (3) For critical brand colors, we can apply a white flood coat under CMYK to achieve true-to-Pantone reproduction. The white underprint adds approximately 10% to unit cost. Pre-production samples let you evaluate the look before committing.

Q: How do vent holes work, and won’t they let the food get cold faster? A: Vent holes are 3–5mm die-cut perforations (circular, slot, or custom logo-shaped) that release steam pressure from hot food, preventing the box from becoming soggy and the window from fogging up. They are strategically placed on the side panels or lid edges — not directly above the food — so convective heat loss is minimal. Our testing shows vented boxes maintain food temperature within 3°C of unvented boxes over a 30-minute delivery window while reducing internal humidity by 40–60%. For cold foods (salads, desserts), we recommend omitting vent holes since steam is not a concern.

Q: What are the minimum order quantities, and can I order small for a pilot launch? A: Standard MOQ is 1,000 units for rectangular/oval windows on standard sizes. Custom window shapes (logo die-cut) and non-standard sizes require 2,000 units minimum due to tooling costs. For restaurant pilot launches, we offer a “micro-batch” program: 500 units at a 25% premium over MOQ pricing, with standard shapes and sizes only. This lets you test customer response and operational fit before committing to full production volumes. Free pre-production samples (5–7 business days) let you evaluate quality before any order commitment.

Q: Are the inks truly food-safe, and what certifications prove it? A: Our soy-based inks are tested to FDA 21 CFR 176.170 (indirect food contact — paper and paperboard components) and EU 1935/2004 (materials intended to come into contact with food). They are free of mineral oil hydrocarbons (MOSH/MOAH) — a critical safety concern for food packaging that many standard offset inks fail. Third-party testing reports from SGS are available for client review. Heavy metals (lead, cadmium, mercury, hexavalent chromium) are below detection limits. For direct food contact (no barrier between ink and food), we recommend the unprinted interior surface — the exterior is printed, the interior remains natural kraft.

Q: What is the lead time and how quickly can I reorder? A: First orders: pre-production samples in 5–7 business days, mass production in 15–20 business days after sample approval. Repeat orders: 10–15 business days (tooling and setup retained). Rush orders (under 5,000 units): 7–10 business days at no surcharge. Our flat-ship design means boxes ship in knocked-down (folded flat) condition — a 20ft container holds approximately 80,000–120,000 units depending on box dimensions, keeping per-unit freight costs low.

Q: Can this box handle greasy or saucy foods without leaking or staining? A: Standard kraft with aqueous coating handles dry/semi-dry foods well but will eventually show oil stains through the board with heavily greased items. For fried foods, saucy meals, or oil-based dressings: (1) Grease-resistant kraft with PFAS-free water-based barrier coating prevents staining for 4+ hours and preserves recyclability — unlike PE-laminated alternatives that must be landfilled. (2) For liquid-heavy foods (curries, soups), we recommend an interior tray liner (PLA or foil) rather than relying on the box itself. (3) Our auto-lock bottom with reinforced corners tested to 1.5kg load — heavier than any typical single-meal order. We recommend the grease-resistant option for any food with visible oil content.
