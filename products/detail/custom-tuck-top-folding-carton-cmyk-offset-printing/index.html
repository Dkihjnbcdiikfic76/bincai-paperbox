Tuck-Top vs. Auto-Lock Bottom: The Weight Threshold Decision

The tuck-top friction closure is reliable up to ~200g product weight. Above that, gravity works against you — the bottom can open during handling. For products over 200g, upgrade to the auto-lock bottom variant: the pre-glued bottom flaps lock automatically when the carton is erected. The unit cost increases ~15–20% but eliminates bottom tape, manual folding, and the risk of product drop. Our structural engineers can send you both samples side-by-side so you can test with your actual product weight.

Quality Assurance

  1. Incoming Material: Board grammage ±5% verification; FSC documentation audit; paper whiteness and opacity per ISO 2470/2471.
  2. Pre-Press Proofing: Digital HP Indigo proof vs. master — color match ΔE ≤ 2.0; die-line alignment verification; trapping check for multi-color overlaps.
  3. Printing (In-Process): X-Rite eXact color check every 500 sheets; Heidelberg Prinect Inpress Control inline spectrophotometry; ink density maintained ±0.05 from target.
  4. Foil Stamping: Temperature maintained ±3°C per zone; pressure uniform across sheet (verified with pressure-sensitive film); 100% visual inspection for foil voids or halos.
  5. Die-Cutting & Creasing: Dimensional accuracy ±0.5mm; crease depth 50% of board thickness for clean fold; 100% strip inspection for hanging chads.
  6. Folding-Gluing: Side-seam bond ≥4N/15mm (T-peel); tuck flap closure force 2–5N (snug but not stiff); 100% inline ejection of open flaps or skewed gluing.
  7. Finished Goods: ANSI/ASQ Z1.4 sampling — AQL 2.5 (major), AQL 1.0 (minor); flatness check (warp ≤2mm over 200mm span); scuff resistance — ≤5% ink transfer after 100 Sutherland double rubs.
  8. ISTA 1A Transit: 10-drop sequence from 760mm — no structural failure; product protection verified.

FAQ

Q: What’s the difference between tuck-top and auto-lock bottom styles?

A: Tuck-top with friction closure is the most economical option — perfect for lightweight products under 200g where gravity keeps the bottom closed. The auto-lock bottom adds pre-glued bottom flaps that automatically lock into position when erected — ideal for heavier products (200g–2kg) or automated filling lines where speed matters. The auto-lock bottom adds approximately 15–20% to the unit cost but eliminates the need for bottom tape or manual folding during filling operations. We can produce both styles and recommend based on your filling process and product weight.

Q: What paper thickness do I need for my product?

A: For lightweight products (cosmetics, tea bags, small electronics accessories): 250–300gsm board. For medium-weight products (chocolate boxes, skincare sets, apparel): 350–400gsm board. For heavy or fragile products (glass bottles, ceramic items, multi-piece kits): 400–450gsm board with reinforced bottom. Our structural design team evaluates your product dimensions, weight, and distribution method to recommend the optimal caliper — we never over-spec or under-spec, because both cost you money.

Q: Can you match my existing packaging exactly?

A: Yes. Send us your existing carton and we’ll reverse-engineer it — measuring board caliper, analyzing paper type, matching colors with spectrophotometer to ΔE ≤ 1.5, and replicating every finish. We’ll produce a pre-production sample for your side-by-side comparison before committing to full production. This service is free with your first order.

Q: How do foil stamping and spot UV affect the per-unit cost?

A: Hot foil stamping adds approximately $0.03–$0.08 per carton depending on foil area and foil type (gold and silver are most economical; holographic and custom-color foils are premium). Spot UV adds $0.02–$0.05 per carton depending on coverage. For a typical 100×60×30mm cosmetics carton, full CMYK + matte lamination + gold foil logo + spot UV accent is approximately $0.30–$0.55/unit at 5,000 units — extremely competitive for premium retail packaging.

Q: What’s the minimum order and how fast can I get samples?

A: MOQ is 500 units for standard sizes using our existing die inventory. For custom sizes requiring new die fabrication, MOQ is 1,000 units. Free pre-production samples (printed and unprinted) are provided in 5–7 working days. Rush sample service available in 3 days for an expedite fee. Q: Are your cartons food-safe?

A: Yes. We offer food-grade configurations with FDA-compliant inks and coatings, water-based adhesives (FDA 21 CFR 175.105 for indirect food contact), and optional inner grease-resistant barrier coatings. For direct food contact (unwrapped chocolates, baked goods), we can add a PE or PLA inner liner. Our food packaging clients include tea brands, chocolate makers, and health food companies across Europe and North America. Full migration testing documentation available on request.

Q: Can you do short runs for seasonal or limited-edition packaging?

A: Yes. Our MOQ of 500 units allows for seasonal packaging runs (Christmas, Valentine’s Day, Lunar New Year) without excessive inventory. For even smaller quantities (100–300 units), we offer digital printing via HP Indigo — slightly higher per-unit cost but zero plate charges, making it economical for influencer kits, PR mailers, and market testing. Digital turnaround is 3–5 days for samples, 7–10 days for production.
