Seven-Stage QC Gates

  5. Board Wrapping & Assembly — Machine-wrapped greyboard panels; magnet cavity routing ±0.3mm precision; N35 neodymium magnet insertion with 100% automated polarity verification (all magnets attract — zero repelling pairs); hinge construction and alignment

FAQ

Q: Will the magnetic closure stay strong after repeated opening and closing — or will it weaken over time?

A: Our magnetic closure is designed for a long service life befitting a premium tea collector’s box — intended to be opened and closed hundreds of times over years of tea enjoyment. We use N35-grade neodymium magnets (the industry standard for luxury packaging), with 4 magnets embedded in the front cover edge for balanced closure force of 4–6N per pair. Each box undergoes automated polarity verification at assembly — ensuring every magnet pair attracts, with zero repelling pairs that would weaken closure. Our hinge cycle testing validates 500+ open/close cycles with no measurable reduction in closure force. For tea brands expecting extremely heavy use (retail display opened dozens of times daily), we recommend upgrading to N42-grade magnets (+5–8% cost) which maintain closure force through 2,000+ cycles.

Q: Can the velvet tray be customized for different tea formats — loose leaf tins, pyramid sachets, mini pu-erh cakes?

A: Absolutely — the tray is the heart of the tea collector’s box and we fully customize it to your specific tea format. Standard options include: (a) Tea bag compartments — individual recessed slots sized for standard rectangular tea bags (60×45mm) or pyramid sachet pouches (50×50mm base), (b) Loose leaf tin cavities — circular recesses for 50g or 100g metal tins, (c) Mini pu-erh cake nests — circular cavities sized for 100g mini cakes (80–100mm diameter), (d) Mixed configuration — combination of tea bag slots, tin cavities, and a central compartment for a tea scoop or tasting notes booklet. Custom compartment configurations require a new die-cut tool (3–5 days, $60–120) and MOQ of 1,000 units. We recommend sending us sample tea items for perfect fit calibration — we’ll return a pre-production sample tray for your approval before full production.

Q: What spine width do I need for my tea collection — and how does it affect the overall box dimensions?

A: Spine width is determined by your tray depth requirement plus 6mm for board thickness (2.5mm × 2 panels + 1mm hinge gap). For a standard 6-compartment tea bag tray (tray depth 15mm), the spine is approximately 21mm wide. For mini pu-erh cakes (tray depth 45mm), the spine is approximately 51mm. As a rule of thumb: measure your thickest tea item, add 3mm for velvet pile clearance, multiply by 1 for single-layer tray, then add 6mm for board — that’s your minimum spine width. We can accommodate spine widths from 30mm (slim tea bag collections) to 80mm (multi-tier pu-erh cake presentation). The exterior cover dimensions are typically 20–30mm larger than the spine width in both directions. Our pre-production sample process includes a dimensional mockup so you can verify fit before tooling.

Q: Can you add a ribbon pull or other closure enhancement for a more ceremonial opening experience?

A: Yes — the ribbon pull is one of our most popular upgrades for tea collector’s boxes and transforms the opening into a ceremonial ritual. We offer: (a) Single satin ribbon pull (10mm or 15mm width, matching or contrasting color) attached to the front cover edge — the consumer lifts the ribbon to open, (b) Dual ribbon tie closure — two ribbons on front and back covers that tie together in a bow (popular for wedding and gift tea sets), (c) Elastic band closure — a branded elastic strap that wraps around the box exterior (modern, minimalist), (d) Belly band — a removable printed paper band that wraps around the closed box with brand messaging and tea collection details. Ribbon pulls add $0.15–0.30/unit; dual ribbon ties add $0.30–0.50/unit. All ribbon materials are available in FSC-certified cotton, recycled polyester, or bamboo fiber for sustainability-conscious brands.
