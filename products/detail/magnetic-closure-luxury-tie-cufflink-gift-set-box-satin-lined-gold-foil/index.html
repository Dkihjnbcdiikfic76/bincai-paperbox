FAQ

Q: Will the magnetic closure hold securely during shipping?

A: Yes. Our N35 neodymium magnets deliver 3.5-5.5 N of closure force — strong enough to keep the lid sealed during international container transit, yet gentle enough for a one-handed opening experience. We’ve shipped over 150,000 magnetic-closure boxes to 50+ countries with zero reported closure failures in transit.

Q: Can the tie be stored pre-tied or rolled?

A: The standard configuration is a full-length flat channel (320mm) that accommodates ties folded once or stored flat. For rolled storage (popular with silk knit ties), we offer a cylindrical insert option that replaces the flat channel. For pre-tied display (common in department store gift sets), we recommend the display channel variant with a clear acetate retention strap — specify during sampling.

Q: What’s the difference between satin and velvet interior lining?

A: Satin (210T polyester) offers a sleek, reflective finish that photographs beautifully for e-commerce and social media. Velvet flocking (450g/m²) provides a richer tactile experience preferred by luxury jewelry and watch brands. Both are OEKO-TEX certified. Most menswear brands choose satin for the tie channel (smooth surface won’t snag silk fibers) and velvet for the cufflink tray (grips accessories in position).

Q: Do you offer rush production for seasonal collections?

A: Yes. Our 18,000 sqm dual-factory setup allows us to run multiple production lines simultaneously. Peak season (August-November for holiday collections) standard lead time is 14-18 days. Rush orders (7-10 days) are available with a 15-20% surcharge. We recommend booking production slots 8 weeks before your launch date to secure capacity.

Q: Can the box accommodate a pocket square alongside the tie and cufflinks?

A: Yes. We offer a 3-compartment configuration: full-length tie channel, 2-position cufflink tray, and a shallow top tray for pocket squares, collar stays, or a handwritten note card. This configuration extends the box depth to approximately 50mm and adds about 10% to the unit cost.
