FAQ

Q: Can the PET window be anti-fog or UV-blocking?

A: Yes. Our standard PET is 0.3mm optical-grade clear film. Anti-fog PET uses a hydrophilic coating that prevents condensation — ideal for refrigerated products or humid retail environments. UV-blocking PET filters ≥95% of UV-A and UV-B radiation, extending product shelf life for light-sensitive goods like chocolate, cosmetics, and natural products. Both options add approximately 8–12% to the window material cost.

Q: How is the satin ribbon attached, and will it come loose during shipping?

A: The ribbon is mechanically secured between the greyboard and the outer wrap paper during the rigid box assembly process — not simply glued on the surface. This sandwich construction means the ribbon is an integral part of the box structure. We pull-test every ribbon at 15N force during QC; failure rate is <0.1%. For pre-tied bows, we use a double-knot with a discreet adhesive tack point that releases cleanly when untied.

Q: Can you match my brand’s Pantone color exactly on the box exterior?

A: Yes. We maintain a full Pantone Formula Guide library and our Heidelberg Speedmaster CD 102 7+1 UV press achieves ΔE <2.0 color matching. For critical brand colors, we provide a wet-proof approval step: you receive a printed sheet of your design on the actual substrate before mass production begins. Spot color matching is included in standard pricing; custom ink mixing for precise brand colors may add a small setup fee.

Q: What’s the difference between soft-touch lamination and matte lamination?

A: Soft-touch lamination (also called velvet lamination) has a tactile, velvety surface that resists fingerprints and scuffs while adding a premium sensory dimension — consumers instinctively want to touch and hold the box. Matte lamination is a flat, non-reflective surface without the tactile component. Both provide excellent scuff resistance. Soft-touch costs approximately 12–18% more than matte but is the preferred choice for luxury gifting where the unboxing experience matters.

Q: Can the box be recycled after use?

A: The greyboard and art paper components are fully recyclable in standard paper/cardboard streams. The PET window should be separated before recycling — we can add a “remove window before recycling” instruction printed on the box interior. The magnets are small (typically 10×3mm or 12×3mm) and do not interfere with most MRF sorting systems. For brands seeking fully recyclable solutions, we offer cellulose-based windows (NatureFlex or equivalent) that are home-compostable, though optical clarity is slightly lower than PET.
