FAQ

Q: Is the glass window safe during shipping? What if it breaks in transit?

A: Our 2mm tempered glass is EN 12150-1 safety certified — it’s 4–5× stronger than annealed glass of the same thickness and, in the unlikely event of breakage, shatters into small granular chunks rather than dangerous shards. We ISTA 1A drop-test every new design (10 drops from 760mm on all faces, edges, and corners) — the glass must survive all 10 drops without cracking. For added protection during long-distance shipping, we offer an anti-shatter film lamination on the glass interior surface that holds fragments in place even if the glass breaks. Field breakage rate across all shipments since 2018 is <0.03%.

Q: Can the watch cushion be made to fit my exact watch model?

A: Absolutely. Send us your watch (we accept shipments to our Foshan factory) or provide detailed CAD measurements, and we’ll CNC-route a custom die-cut cushion to ±0.5mm of your watch case dimensions. The cushion cavity includes a channel for the watch band to wrap beneath, finger-access notches for easy removal, and appropriate depth so the crystal sits flush with or slightly below the cushion surface. For brands with multiple watch models, we can create a master die that accommodates all case sizes within a range (e.g., 38–42mm). Cushion samples ship within 5–7 days for your approval.

Q: How does the magnetic closure compare to a hinge in terms of durability and feel?

A: Both have excellent durability — our N52 magnetic closure is rated for 100,000+ cycles (magnet strength degrades <1% over 20 years), while the stainless steel hinge is rated for 5,000+ cycles with zero play development. The magnetic closure provides a satisfying “snap” close with adjustable resistance (1.5–3.0kg pull force, tuned by magnet count and positioning) — popular for modern and minimalist watch brands. The metal hinge provides a traditional jewelry-box feel with controlled opening angle (95° stop) — preferred by heritage and dress watch brands. For the ultimate luxury experience, we can combine both: magnetic closure for the satisfying snap, with a ribbon or hinge for controlled opening.

Q: Can you do anti-tarnish or humidity-controlled features for high-value mechanical watches?

A: Yes. For mechanical watch storage (especially vintage pieces sensitive to humidity), we offer: (1) anti-tarnish silvercloth lining — impregnated with activated silver particles that absorb atmospheric sulfur compounds, preventing tarnish on silver and gold-plated watches; (2) silica gel desiccant compartment integrated into the box base — maintains <40% RH inside the closed box, replaceable cartridge; (3) activated carbon filter layer for odor and VOC absorption; (4) UV-filter glass window option — blocks 99% of UV radiation ≤400nm, protecting watch dials and straps from fading. These features are standard for our collector-grade watch boxes.

Q: What’s the minimum order and how fast can I get a prototype?

A: MOQ is 500 units for standard sizes and materials. For fully custom dimensions, materials, or specialty features (UV glass, anti-tarnish lining), MOQ is 1,000 units. Free pre-production samples — including a physical prototype with your watch — are provided in 5–7 working days. The prototype includes your chosen materials, finishes, glass window, and cushion — a complete representation of the final product. We recommend ordering 2–3 prototypes with different material combinations to compare before full production.

Q: Can the glass window be customized with brand etching or printing?

A: Yes. We offer: (1) sandblast etching — your logo or pattern etched into the glass surface (interior side to prevent finger contact), matte white appearance, permanent and scratch-proof; (2) UV digital printing on glass — full-color logo or pattern with ±0.1mm registration, cured under UV for durability; (3) screen-printed ceramic frit — metallic gold/silver/white border or logo, fired at 600°C for permanent bonding. Glass customization adds approximately $0.50–$1.50/unit depending on process and coverage area. Samples provided in 7–10 days for your approval.

Q: Does the velvet cushion shed fibers onto the watch over time?

A: Our premium velvet undergoes a three-stage anti-shed finishing process — precision shearing to uniform 1.0–1.2mm pile height, anti-static treatment to prevent fiber pull-out, and heat-setting at 140°C to lock fibers. ASTM D4966 Martindale abrasion testing confirms <1% pile loss after 20,000+ rub cycles. For brands with extreme sensitivity to any fiber transfer (polished platinum, exhibition casebacks), we recommend our microfiber suede cushion option — identical luxurious appearance with zero fiber shedding, commonly used by Swiss watch brands for their presentation boxes.
