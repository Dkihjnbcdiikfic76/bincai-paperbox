Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is the gable-top box food-safe for direct contact with unwrapped food? Our SBS board and barrier coatings comply with FDA 21 CFR 176.170 (US) and EU 1935/2004 (Europe) for indirect food contact. For direct contact with fatty or moist foods, we recommend the PE or PLA barrier-coated interior. Full migration testing reports available through SGS or Intertek upon request — standard turnaround 5-7 working days.

Q: What’s the difference between PE and PLA compostable coating? PE (polyethylene) is the industry standard for grease and moisture resistance — it’s food-safe but the coated box must go to standard paper recycling (not composting). PLA (polylactic acid) is a bio-based compostable alternative made from corn starch — the entire box is EN 13432 certified for industrial composting. PLA is slightly more expensive (+15-20%) but preferred by zero-waste and organic brands.

Q: How strong is the gable handle? The integrated gable handle is tested to ≥2kg static load — easily carries a box of pastries, chocolates, or a meal for two. For heavier contents (bottles, jars, hampers), we recommend upgrading to a twisted paper cord handle (3mm, 5kg rated) or die-cut slot with reinforced tabs.

Q: Can I have a window so customers see the food inside? Yes — we offer die-cut open windows, clear PET film (0.3mm), anti-fog PET for refrigerated bakery displays, and compostable cellulose film (NatureFlex™). The window can be arched, circular, rectangular, or custom-shaped to frame your product beautifully. Anti-fog PET is critical for products displayed in chilled cabinets.

Q: What are the MOQ and lead time for a custom gable-top box? MOQ is 1,000 units for standard print; 3,000+ for multi-process (foil stamping + window + barrier coating). Lead time is 7-15 working days for tooling preparation plus 15-25 days production depending on quantity. Rush service available: 7-10 days total with surcharge. Free structural design and 3D mockup within 24 hours.

Q: Can the box be recycled after use? Yes — SBS board is fully recyclable in standard paper streams. PE-coated boxes are recyclable (the coating is removed during repulping). PLA-coated boxes can go to industrial composting facilities (EN 13432). We recommend printing recycling/disposal instructions on the box bottom to guide consumers. All paper is FSC Chain-of-Custody certified.
