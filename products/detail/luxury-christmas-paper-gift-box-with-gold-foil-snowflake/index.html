FAQ

Q: Can the snowflake pattern be changed to a different seasonal or custom design? A: Yes. The snowflake pattern is created via a custom brass hot foil stamping die, which we manufacture in-house. You can replace the snowflake with any design — holly leaves, stars, company logo, monogram, geometric patterns, or any vector artwork. Die creation takes 3–5 business days and the setup cost is waived on orders over 3,000 units.

Q: What is the difference between hot foil stamping and gold ink printing? A: Hot foil stamping uses heat, pressure, and a metallic foil film to transfer a genuine metallic layer onto the box surface — it produces a reflective, dimensional finish with actual metallic sheen that catches and reflects light. Gold ink printing (offset or screen) uses metallic pigment suspended in ink — it is uniform and flat, without the reflective brilliance of foil. For luxury packaging where the unboxing experience matters, hot foil stamping is the industry standard. We recommend foil for logos, patterns, and branding elements; ink for large-area coverage where foil cost would be prohibitive.

Q: What interior lining options are available, and how are they attached? A: We offer satin (smooth, slight sheen), velvet (soft, plush, matte), flocked paper (textured, cost-effective), and suede-look (premium matte) linings in any Pantone-matched color. Linings are precision die-cut to fit the box interior and attached with water-based adhesive. For jewelry and watch boxes, we add a padded insert layer beneath the lining for extra cushioning. Custom die-cut cavities for specific products are available — provide your product dimensions and we create a form-fitted insert.

Q: Can these boxes be used for non-Christmas gifting by changing the design? A: Absolutely. While this listing showcases the snowflake-themed holiday design, the same rigid box construction is available year-round with any foil pattern, color scheme, or branding. Replace the snowflake with a floral pattern for spring, a geometric design for corporate gifting, a wedding monogram for bridal favors, or a company logo for year-round client appreciation — the box structure, quality, and production process remain identical.
