FAQ

Q: Is this box truly recyclable in curbside programs — or does it require special processing?

A: The box is 100% curbside recyclable in standard paper/cardboard streams. We achieve this by eliminating mixed-material lamination — there is no plastic film, no foil lamination, and no petroleum-based coating on the kraft surface. The water-based PVA adhesive and EVA hot-melt are both recyclable-compatible (< 1% of total box weight) and dissolve during the repulping process without contaminating the fiber stream. The recycled neodymium magnets are paper-over-wrapped and can be removed during pulping (magnetic separation). We verify recyclability per TAPPI T-275 with ≥ 95% fiber recovery. Note: if you choose the compostable PLA window option, the consumer should separate the window from the box before recycling — we include clear separation instructions on the box. The PLA window is compostable in industrial facilities per EN 13432.

Q: How does soy-based ink perform on kraft paper — won’t the colors look muddy compared to printing on white art paper?

A: This is the single most common technical concern with kraft printing, and it’s one we’ve solved through years of substrate-specific calibration. Printing on brown kraft requires adjusted ink density curves — we increase ink film thickness by approximately 15–20% compared to white substrates and use a white under-base layer for CMYK builds when color vibrancy is critical. The result is rich, saturated color that leverages the warmth of the kraft background rather than fighting it. Our Heidelberg Inpress Control spectrophotometer monitors color density in real-time every 500 sheets, maintaining ΔE ≤ 2.0 throughout the run. For brands needing maximum color pop, we recommend: (a) white kraft instead of natural brown, (b) black-only or 2-color designs for a sophisticated minimalist aesthetic, or (c) hot foil stamping (matte gold, copper) which reads brilliantly on kraft and aligns with the eco-luxury aesthetic.

Q: Can you provide full documentation to support our brand’s sustainability marketing claims?

A: Yes — we provide a comprehensive sustainability documentation package with every order: FSC chain-of-custody certificate (independently audited annually), soy-based ink certification (heavy-metal free, >20% bio-based content), recycled greyboard content certification (85%+ post-consumer), recycled PET flocking supplier declaration, EN 13432 compostability certificate (if PLA window option selected), recyclability test report per TAPPI T-275, ISO 9001:2015 certificate, and a factory sustainability statement documenting our 98% paper waste recycling rate, water treatment system, and energy efficiency initiatives. This documentation supports your own sustainability marketing, ESG reporting, and regulatory compliance in EU, UK, and North American markets.

Q: What happens to the magnets during recycling — won’t they contaminate the paper stream?

A: Neodymium magnets are ferromagnetic and are captured by the magnetic separation stage that is standard in all commercial paper recycling facilities — the same process that removes staples, paper clips, and other ferrous contaminants. The magnets are paper-over-wrapped (0.3mm kraft cover), so they separate cleanly from the fiber during the pulping stage. The paper over-wrap dissolves, releasing the magnet which is then removed by the magnetic belt. For facilities without magnetic separation, the small magnet size (8mm diameter) means it passes through the screening stage and is removed with other small non-fiber contaminants. This is a solved problem in commercial recycling — our TAPPI T-275 testing confirms ≥ 95% fiber recovery with no magnet-related contamination.

Q: Can we do a fully plastic-free version of this box — including the interior?

A: Yes — we offer a “Zero Plastic” variant that replaces every synthetic component: the interior tray becomes molded recycled pulp (compostable, cradle-to-cradle certified), the velvet flocking is omitted in favor of unbleached organic cotton canvas or textured kraft lining, and the magnetic closure uses paper-based magnetic sheet (ferrite particles in a paper matrix — fully recyclable) instead of neodymium magnets. The closure can also be a cotton ribbon tie, hemp twine wrap, or bamboo toggle-and-loop — all 100% biodegradable. The Zero Plastic variant carries a small premium (+15–20%) due to material costs, and MOQ is 1,000 units. This option is particularly popular with B Corp certified fragrance brands and those targeting the EU Green Claims Directive.
