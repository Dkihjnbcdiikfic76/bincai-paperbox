FAQ

Q: Is double-wall really necessary, or can I use single-wall for lighter products?

A: For products under 500g (1 lb) and shipped via ground, single-wall B-flute corrugated is often sufficient and costs 25–35% less. However, double-wall BC-flute becomes cost-effective when you factor in total cost of ownership: lower damage rates, fewer replacement shipments, fewer customer service inquiries, and better brand protection. Our analysis of customer shipping data shows that switching from single-wall to double-wall typically reduces product damage claims by 60–80% while adding only $0.10–0.30 per box. For products over 1.5kg or shipped via air (more handling), double-wall is strongly recommended.

Q: How does the self-seal adhesive hold up in hot or cold shipping conditions?

A: Our hot-melt pressure-sensitive adhesive is formulated for a broad temperature range: -20°C to 60°C (-4°F to 140°F). It maintains adhesion in freezer-to-ambient transitions (frozen food subscription boxes) and does not soften or ooze in hot warehouse or truck conditions (up to 60°C). We test adhesion at both temperature extremes: peel strength remains above 2.5 lbs/inch at -20°C and above 2.0 lbs/inch at 60°C. For extreme cold-chain applications (dry ice shipping, -40°C and below), we offer a specialty cryogenic-grade PSA.

Q: Can I print full-color branding on corrugated like I can on folding cartons?

A: Yes, via litho-lamination. We print your full-color design on 157gsm art paper using our Heidelberg CD 102 7+1 UV offset press, then laminate it to the corrugated board. This gives you photo-quality printing on the exterior while the corrugated structure provides shipping protection. Litho-lamination adds approximately 40–60% to unit cost compared to flexo printing and extends lead time by 5–7 days. For simpler branding (logo + 1–2 colors), flexo printing is more economical and faster.

Q: Does the tear strip actually work reliably, or will customers still need scissors?

A: The tear strip is engineered and tested to tear cleanly in a single continuous pull. Our PET mono-filament (2mm width) is positioned between the outer and inner liners during corrugation — it becomes an integral part of the board structure. During gluing, the tear strip end is left protruding with a pull-tab. We 100% functionally test: the strip must tear its full length without breaking, snagging, or deviating from the intended tear path. Consumer feedback from our e-commerce brand customers shows >99% successful one-pull opening, with the last <1% typically occurring when users pull at extreme angles rather than straight back.

Q: Are the boxes recyclable?

A: Yes. Self-seal corrugated mailers are fully recyclable in standard curbside paper/cardboard programs. The hot-melt adhesive strip and PET tear strip are present in such small quantities that they do not interfere with repulping — they are screened out as contaminants during the recycling process. We recommend adding a “Please Recycle This Box” message printed on the interior flap to encourage consumer recycling. FSC certification (FSC-C147399) verifies responsible fiber sourcing through the supply chain.
