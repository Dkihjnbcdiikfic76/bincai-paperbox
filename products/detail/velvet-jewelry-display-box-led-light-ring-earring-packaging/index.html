FAQ

Q: How long does the LED battery last, and is it replaceable? A: Battery life depends on the power configuration you choose. CR2032 coin cell provides 48–72 hours of continuous illumination (sufficient for 5,000+ open/close cycles in retail use). AAA (2×) provides 120–180 hours. USB-C rechargeable Li-ion (200mAh) lasts 60–80 hours per charge and recharges in 45 minutes via any standard USB port. All battery types are user-replaceable — the battery compartment is accessible via a hidden flap in the base. For retail display that stays open all day, we recommend the USB rechargeable option.

Q: Can the LED color temperature be customized? A: Yes. Standard options are warm white (3000K — flattering for yellow gold and rose gold jewelry), neutral white (4000K — accurate for diamonds and white gold), and cool white (6000K — crisp for silver and platinum). We also offer RGB LED modules with remote control for dynamic color-changing displays — ideal for trade show booths, window displays, and experiential retail. Custom color temperature matching is available for orders of 2,000+ units.

Q: How is the LED activated when the box opens? A: We use a magnetic reed switch embedded in the box lid and base. When the magnetic lid is closed, the reed switch breaks the circuit (LED off). When the box is opened, the magnet separates from the reed switch, completing the circuit and illuminating the LED instantly. This mechanism has been tested to 10,000+ open/close cycles with zero failures. Alternative activation methods include touch sensor (tap-to-light) and manual push-button switch — both available on request.

Q: Is the velvet flocking safe for jewelry — will it scratch delicate pieces? A: Absolutely. Our nylon velvet flocking has a pile height of 1.0–1.5mm with rounded fiber tips, creating a soft cushion that protects fine jewelry from scratches. It has been independently tested for abrasion resistance (500+ rub cycles, ASTM D3884) and passes the “silk test” — dragging a silk scarf across the flocked surface leaves zero snags or pulls. For extra-delicate items like pearls or opals, we offer an ultra-fine 0.5mm suede microfiber option.

Q: What is the minimum order for custom die-cut foam inserts? A: CNC die-cut foam inserts require tooling with a one-time cost of $80–150 depending on cavity complexity. The minimum order for custom insert tooling is 1,000 units. For orders under 1,000, we offer universal-fit inserts (ring slot, earring card, necklace hook) that accommodate standard jewelry dimensions without tooling cost.
