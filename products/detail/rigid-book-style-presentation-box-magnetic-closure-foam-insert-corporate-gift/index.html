FAQ

Q: How does the book-style magnetic closure work, and will it stay closed during shipping? A: The book-style box uses two neodymium disc magnets (typically 8-12mm diameter) embedded between the greyboard layers in both the lid and base panels. The magnets are precisely aligned so that when the lid closes, they attract with a satisfying snap and hold the lid securely. We test every design with a 50cm drop test and vibration simulation — the magnetic closure maintains integrity even after simulated transit conditions. For extra security, we can add a magnetic flap with a second pair of magnets or an elastic closure band.

Q: Can the foam insert be customized for multiple different products in the same box? A: Yes. Our CNC foam cutting process can create multi-cavity layouts that hold multiple items in a single tray — popular for corporate kits containing a pen, notebook, USB drive, and welcome card all in one presentation box. Each cavity is die-cut to the exact dimensions of its specific item. We can also do multi-layer foam trays (stacked) for tiered unboxing where the recipient discovers items layer by layer.

Q: What is the minimum order quantity for a fully custom book-style box with foam insert? A: Standard MOQ is 300-500 units depending on complexity and material specifications. The foam insert tooling (CNC die) is a one-time cost of approximately $50-150 depending on cavity complexity, and this tooling is yours to keep for reorders. For trial orders or market testing, we can produce as few as 100 units with a small-run surcharge.

Q: How long does it take from design approval to delivery? A: Standard production timeline is 18-25 working days from artwork approval: 5-7 days for pre-production samples (free, express courier), 10-15 days for mass production, and 3-5 days for ocean freight consolidation or express air shipping. Rush orders can be completed in 10-12 working days with expedited service.

Q: What makes Bincai’s rigid box corners superior to other suppliers? A: Our rigid box corner construction uses a proprietary three-step process: (1) greyboard panels are precision-cut on a fully automatic rigid box machine with ±0.3mm tolerance, (2) each corner is folded using hot-melt adhesive with controlled temperature and pressure to eliminate glue seepage, and (3) wrapped corners are inspected under 3× magnification for any visible greyboard edge or paper lifting. The result is a perfectly sharp, clean corner that looks indistinguishable from a one-piece molded construction — a quality level typically found only in boxes retailing for $15-30 at luxury department stores.

Sustainability

Bincai is committed to environmentally responsible rigid box manufacturing:

  • FSC Certified Paper — All greyboard and wrap paper used in our rigid boxes is sourced from FSC-certified forests and controlled sources.

  • Recyclable Construction — Our rigid boxes use paper-based greyboard core and paper wrap — fully recyclable in standard paper waste streams. Magnetic closures are embedded and do not interfere with the recycling process.

  • Soy-Based Inks — Soy-based and vegetable-based offset inks produce vibrant colors with lower VOC emissions and easier de-inking during recycling.

  • Water-Based Adhesives — Hot-melt and water-based adhesives replace solvent-based glues for box assembly, reducing VOC emissions in our factory and making the finished box easier to recycle.

  • Efficient Flat Shipping — Rigid boxes are pre-assembled and nested for shipping. We optimize pallet configuration to maximize container density, reducing carbon footprint per unit.
