Guangdong Bincai Color Printing Co., Ltd. produces this custom-printed corrugated e-commerce subscription box engineered for direct-to-consumer (DTC) brands shipping monthly boxes. The B-flute single-wall construction provides crush protection for 1-5kg payloads while the integrated tear strip — a perforated zip-open feature on the top panel — lets consumers open the box without tools, preserving the unboxing experience. Full-color printing on the exterior turns the shipping box into a brand touchpoint, eliminating the need for an inner gift box. Manufactured on our BHS 2.5m corrugator and printed via flexo or litho-laminate (KBA 1050 + Heidelberg 7+1 UV for photo-quality graphics) in our 18,000 sqm Foshan facility, each box passes Seven-Stage QC with ISO 9001:2015 and FSC Chain-of-Custody certification. Daily output of 1.7 million boxes supports seasonal volume spikes common in subscription businesses.

Manufacturing Detail

Board is produced in-house on the BHS 2.5m-width corrugator running at 250 m/min — the widest corrugating line in the Pearl River Delta region. Standard board construction is B-flute single-wall (125 gsm kraft liner / 112 gsm fluting medium / 125 gsm kraft liner) yielding a 3 mm caliper with ≥32 lb/in ECT. For heavier payloads (5-10 kg), we upgrade to C-flute single-wall (4 mm) or BC double-wall (6 mm). Printing is performed on either a flexographic station (2-3 spot colors, water-based inks, 120 lpi) or a litho-laminate line where sheets are printed on the KBA Rapida 105 (4-color process, 175 lpi, ±0.15 mm registration) then UV-coated on the Heidelberg Speedmaster CD 102 (7+1 configuration) before being laminated to the corrugated board with a hot-melt adhesive. The tear strip perforation is applied inline during converting on our Bobst die-cutter, eliminating secondary handling. Boxes are folder-glued with cold-set PVA adhesive, flat-packed, and banded 25-50 per bundle for palletization. The entire process — from roll stock to palletized cartons — is completed within 18,000 sqm of contiguous production floor space by our team of 350+ workers, supporting a daily throughput of 1.7 million finished boxes.

Every corrugated subscription box passes through seven mandatory quality control gates from raw material intake to palletized shipment. Non-conforming product at any gate triggers a hold-release protocol: the batch is quarantined, root-cause analysis is completed within 24 hours, and corrective action is verified before the lot is released downstream.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for custom subscription boxes?

Our MOQ is 500 units for flexographic printing and 1,000 units for litho-laminate (offset) printing. This low MOQ is ideal for subscription box startups testing the market or established DTC brands running limited-edition seasonal boxes. Sample orders (5–10 units) are available free of charge before committing to production. For orders above 50,000 units, we offer volume-tiered pricing with further cost savings of 8–12%.

How does the tear strip work, and can it be customized?

The tear strip is a perforated line cut inline during the converting process on our Bobst die-cutter — no secondary operation or added cost. When the consumer pulls the strip, the top panel separates cleanly for tool-free opening. We offer three configurations: (1) single perforated strip across the top panel (standard), (2) dual side strips for full-top removal, and (3) zipper-style adhesive tear tape for a premium feel. The perforation depth is calibrated to ensure clean separation without board tearing, verified by 100% manual pull testing at QC Gate G5.

What print quality can I expect on corrugated board?

We offer two print tiers. Flexographic printing (2–3 colors, water-based inks, 120 lpi) is cost-effective for bold logos, brand colors, and simple graphics — suitable for most subscription boxes. Litho-laminate printing uses our KBA Rapida 105 4-color offset press (175 lpi, ±0.15 mm registration) plus Heidelberg Speedmaster CD 102 7+1 UV to produce photo-quality graphics on a clay-coated liner that is then laminated to the corrugated board. Litho-lam delivers folding-carton-level print quality with corrugated-level crush protection — ideal for premium beauty and lifestyle subscription brands.

How long does production and shipping take?

Custom orders require 10–14 days production plus 12–16 days sea freight to US West Coast (approximately 4 weeks door-to-door). Repeat orders with existing tooling ship in 5–7 days production time. Pre-production samples are dispatched within 5–7 days via air courier at no charge. We recommend placing orders 6–8 weeks before your subscription box ship date to allow for proofing, production, ocean transit, and customs clearance.

Are these boxes eco-friendly and recyclable?

Yes. Our corrugated subscription boxes are 100% curbside recyclable as OCC (Old Corrugated Containers). We offer 30–100% recycled fiber content, FSC Chain-of-Custody certified virgin fiber, water-based inks with VOC below 5%, and PVA adhesive that is water-soluble and repulpable. The standard tear strip uses paper-based perforation (no plastic tape), making the box fully plastic-free. Post-production board trim is re-pulped on-site in our closed-loop recycling system and fed back into the corrugator. We comply with EU Packaging Directive 94/62/EC, REACH, and California Proposition 65.
