Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do the seed-embedded tags actually work? A: Each tag is made from recycled cotton paper pulp embedded with non-GMO wildflower seeds (typically a mix of 5–8 varieties including poppy, daisy, cornflower, and black-eyed Susan). After unboxing, the customer soaks the tag in water for 4–6 hours, places it in soil (1cm deep), waters regularly, and seedlings emerge within 7–14 days. Full blooms appear in 6–10 weeks depending on variety and climate. Planting instructions are printed directly on the tag with soy ink.

Q: Is the entire box compostable — including the ink and adhesive? A: Yes. The entire box structure meets ASTM D6400 compostability: FSC kraft paperboard (backbone), soy-based inks (≤3% VOC), water-based adhesive, and compostable PLA window film (if selected). The seed tag and cotton/jute handles are also fully biodegradable. We recommend industrial composting for fastest results, but home composting works too (may take 6–12 months vs 180 days).

Q: What seed varieties are available for the tag? A: Standard options: Wildflower Mix (poppy, daisy, cornflower, black-eyed Susan, calendula — suitable worldwide), Herb Mix (basil, thyme, oregano, chives — great for food brands), Tree Mix (pine or spruce — for larger-scale environmental branding), and Custom Blends (your brand’s signature flower mix — minimum 5,000 tags). All seeds are non-GMO and phytosanitary certified for international export.

Q: Can the box handle heavier products like glass jars or bottles? A: Yes. Our 400gsm kraft paperboard with reinforced auto-lock bottom construction supports products up to 1.5kg. For heavier items (>1.5kg), we recommend double-wall kraft construction or adding a kraft corrugated insert. Box Compression Test (BCT) rating ≥150N for standard sizes, ≥250N for reinforced versions.

Q: How does the printing quality compare to conventional laminated boxes? A: Soy-based offset ink on uncoated kraft produces a natural, matte, tactile aesthetic — intentionally different from glossy laminated boxes. Color saturation is approximately 85–90% of coated paper equivalent, which aligns with the “authentic eco” brand positioning. For brands needing higher color vibrancy, we offer an aqueous matte coating that boosts saturation to ~95% while maintaining compostability. Blind embossing and debossing add premium texture without any coating.

Q: What’s the MOQ and pricing compared to conventional packaging? A: MOQ is 1,000 units per design. Unit pricing ranges from $0.50–2.50 depending on size, complexity, and quantity. This is typically 15–25% higher than conventional PE-laminated boxes due to FSC-certified paper and seed tag handcrafting costs. However, brands often recoup this premium through improved sustainability marketing, eco-conscious consumer loyalty, and compliance with emerging plastic packaging regulations (EU PPWR, California SB 54).

Q: Do you provide biodegradability and compostability certificates for customs/retailers? A: Yes. We provide full documentation packages: ASTM D6400 test reports, FSC chain-of-custody certificates, seed phytosanitary certificates, soy ink VOC compliance statements, and plastic-free declarations. These documents satisfy EU REACH, US FTC Green Guides, and major retailer sustainability audit requirements.
