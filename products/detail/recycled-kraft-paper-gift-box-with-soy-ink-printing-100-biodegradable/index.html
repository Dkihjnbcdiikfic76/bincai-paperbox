FAQ

Q: Is this box truly compostable at home, or only in industrial facilities? A: Our standard configuration (100% PCR kraft + soy ink + aqueous coating) is certified to ASTM D6400 for industrial composting, breaking down within 6–12 weeks. For home compostability, we offer an uncoated configuration with starch-based adhesive and no window film — this degrades within 3–6 months in a home compost pile. During inquiry, specify “home compostable” and we’ll configure materials accordingly.

Q: Will the soy-based ink smudge or fade in humid bathroom or retail environments? A: No. Soy-based offset ink is cured at high temperature during printing and is as durable as conventional petroleum-based inks. Combined with our aqueous coating (water-based, recyclable), the print resists humidity and handling. We test to ≥500 Sutherland double rubs without surface change. For extreme moisture exposure (shower steam, tropical climates), we recommend upgrading to our biodegradable barrier coating configuration.

Q: Can I display the FSC and compostable certification logos on this box? A: Yes. As an FSC Chain-of-Custody certified manufacturer (FSC-STD-40-004 v3-0), Bincai can print the FSC Recycled or FSC Mix logo directly on your boxes, subject to your brand’s FSC trademark license. The ASTM D6400 industrial compostable logo is also available for on-box printing. Both logos are powerful consumer trust signals — particularly for skincare, cosmetics, and food brands where sustainability claims are scrutinized.

Q: How does the cost compare to conventional plastic-laminated gift boxes? A: Our PCR kraft + soy ink + aqueous coating configuration is typically price-competitive with mid-range conventional boxes (CCNB + petroleum ink + gloss BOPP lamination). The cost premium for full eco-certification is approximately 10–20% over basic CCNB boxes, but this is often offset by: (1) 80–90% freight volume savings versus rigid boxes (kraft folding cartons ship flat), (2) elimination of plastic packaging taxes in EU/UK markets, and (3) premium brand positioning that commands higher retail prices. A 40ft container holds ~5× more flat-packed kraft boxes than assembled rigid boxes — dramatically reducing per-unit landed cost.

Q: What’s the difference between PCR (post-consumer recycled), FSC Mix, and virgin FSC kraft? A: 100% PCR = paper made entirely from consumer-recycled waste (cereal boxes, office paper, etc.) — maximum circular economy impact. FSC Mix = a blend of FSC-certified virgin fiber + recycled material + controlled wood — widely available, good availability. FSC 100% = 100% FSC-certified virgin fiber from responsibly managed forests — best for brands that want pure FSC messaging. Virgin FSC kraft = strongest and most consistent surface for printing but lowest recycled content. Most eco-positioned brands choose PCR or FSC Mix.

Q: Can you do soy-based foil stamping for a fully compostable metallic finish? A: Yes. We offer soy-based metallic foil alternatives to traditional petroleum-based foils. While slightly less brilliant than conventional hot stamping foil, soy-based foils maintain full compostability and deliver a subtle, organic metallic effect that aligns with natural brand aesthetics.
