FAQ

Q: Is the PET window food-safe for direct tea contact?

A: The PET window film itself is food-grade and complies with EU 10/2011 for food contact materials. The hot-melt adhesive used to bond the window meets FDA 21 CFR 175.105 for indirect food contact. However, for tea sold in direct contact with the window (e.g., loose-leaf tea touching the film), we recommend an inner food-grade pouch — which we can source and include in-line during carton assembly.

Q: What’s the minimum order quantity for a fully custom size?

A: MOQ is 1,000 units for a fully custom size requiring new cutting and creasing dies. For sizes within our existing die library (60×40×100mm to 120×80×200mm range), MOQ drops to 500 units with no die charge. Our in-house die-making team can produce a new die in 3-5 days — included at no cost for orders of 5,000+ units.

Q: Can the soft-touch laminate handle tea oils and moisture?

A: Yes. The soft-touch matte lamination is a 25-micron polypropylene film with anti-scuff and moisture-resistant properties. It withstands incidental contact with tea oils and ambient humidity without peeling or clouding. For tea brands selling in humid climates (Southeast Asia, coastal regions), we recommend upgrading to the 35-micron film — adds approximately 8% to unit cost but provides superior moisture barrier performance.

Q: Do you offer window shapes beyond rectangular?

A: Absolutely. Our die-cutting department can create windows in any vector shape: tea leaf silhouettes, brand logos, circular portholes, or arched cathedral windows. Complex shapes (sharp corners, narrow bridges) may require ±0.5mm tolerance vs. the standard ±0.3mm for rectangular windows. Submit your vector artwork (AI, EPS, or PDF) during sampling and we’ll validate manufacturability within 24 hours.

Q: Can the carton be designed for flat shipping and in-store assembly?

A: Yes. The tuck-top design ships flat and folds into final form in under 10 seconds. For tea brands that fill cartons in-house, we can skip the window patching step and ship flat cartons with pre-scored fold lines — you apply your own inner pouch and fold the carton closed. This reduces shipping volume by 85% vs. pre-assembled cartons but adds an in-house assembly step.
