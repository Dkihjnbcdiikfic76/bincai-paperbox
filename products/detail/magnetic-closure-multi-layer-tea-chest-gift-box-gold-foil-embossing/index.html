Product Overview

The Bincai Multi-Layer Tea Chest with Gold Foil Embossing reimagines the traditional tea cabinet as a precision-engineered luxury gift box. Designed for premium tea brands, boutique tea houses, and corporate gifting programs, this 3-tier magnetic closure chest delivers an unboxing experience that communicates heritage, craftsmanship, and exclusivity before the first tea leaf is seen.

Each of the three satin-lined drawers slides on precision die-cut tracks (±0.2mm tolerance) engineered on our Bobst Visioncut 106 LER flatbed die-cutter. The drawers are independently secured by concealed N52-grade neodymium magnets — the highest commercially available grade — providing a consistent 800g+ holding force tested on every unit. The book-style lid opens to reveal a gold foil embossed brand plate, while brass-finish drawer pulls (customizable to antique bronze, chrome, rose gold, or matte black) add vintage character.

The chest body is constructed from FSC-certified greyboard (1200–1600gsm, wrapped in 157gsm art paper) with 1.5mm–3.0mm board thickness for substantial weight and durability. Gold foil hot stamping is applied on our Bobst SP 102 BMA/E autoplaten, achieving ±0.1mm registration for crisp logo detail and decorative borders. All production runs through Bincai’s 18,000 sqm integrated facility in Foshan, Guangdong, under ISO 9001:2015 quality management and FSC-C147399 chain-of-custody certification.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can the tea chest be configured with 4 or 5 drawers instead of 3? A: Yes, the drawer count is fully customizable from 2 to 6 tiers. Each additional tier increases the overall height by approximately 45–55mm depending on board thickness (1.5mm–3.0mm). For 5+ drawer configurations, we recommend upgrading to 1600gsm greyboard for structural stability. Tooling adjustments for non-standard drawer counts add approximately 3–5 days to sample turnaround and may incur a modest tooling fee (waived for orders above 2,000 units).

Q: What’s the difference between N35 and N52 magnets, and why use N52? A: N35 and N52 are grades on the neodymium magnet scale, where higher numbers indicate stronger magnetic field. N52 is the strongest commercially available grade — approximately 40% stronger than N35. For a multi-drawer tea chest, N52 magnets ensure each drawer’s 800g+ holding force remains consistent across thousands of open-close cycles without degradation. N35 may suffice for small, lightweight boxes (under 500g per drawer), but for a premium tea chest where the satisfying magnetic “snap” is part of the luxury experience, N52 is the standard. We stock N35, N42, N48, and N52 grades to match your budget and performance requirements.

Q: How does the tea chest hold up during international shipping and transit? A: Every tea chest passes ISTA 1A drop testing — 10 drops from 60cm height across all corners, edges, and faces. The precision-fit drawer tracks (±0.2mm) prevent drawers from shifting during transit. For bulk shipping, each chest is individually wrapped in acid-free tissue, placed in a 5-layer corrugated master carton with corner protectors, and palletized at 48–64 units per standard pallet. A 20ft container holds approximately 2,800–3,200 units depending on chest dimensions. We have shipped tea chests to over 40 countries with a transit damage rate below 0.3%.

Q: What is the sample and production lead time? A: Pre-production samples with your logo and chosen configuration ship within 5–7 business days — free of charge (you cover express shipping only). After sample approval, mass production lead time is 15–20 business days for orders up to 5,000 units. Rush orders (under 2,000 units) can be completed in 10–12 business days at no surcharge. Repeat orders ship in 10–15 days since tooling and setup are retained.

Q: Can I mix different tea varieties in the same chest with different compartment layouts per drawer? A: Absolutely. Each of the three drawers can have independently configured die-cut insert dividers — for example, Drawer 1 with 6 compartments for tea bags, Drawer 2 with 2 compartments for loose-leaf tins, and Drawer 3 with 4 compartments for tea accessories. The insert dividers are made from the same satin-lined greyboard as the drawer body and are fully customizable for compartment count, divider height, and shape (square, rectangular, circular cutouts for tins). We can also incorporate removable divider trays for flexible use.

Q: Is the gold foil embossing durable, and does it tarnish over time? A: The gold foil we use is genuine hot-stamped metallic foil (Kurz Luxor series) applied at 110–130°C and 2–4 tons of pressure on our Bobst SP 102 BMA/E. The bond is permanent — not a surface print or transfer. Our abrasion resistance testing (Sutherland Rub Test, 500 cycles at 2kg pressure) confirms no visible wear. The foil does not tarnish or oxidize because it’s a metallic pigment layer sealed between a polyester carrier and a lacquer topcoat. For extremely humid environments (tropical storage), we recommend adding a UV-cured protective overcoat for additional moisture resistance.

Q: What certifications does Bincai hold, and are they audited by third parties? A: Bincai holds ISO 9001:2015 (quality management), FSC-C147399 (forest stewardship chain-of-custody), and Sedex SMETA 4-Pillar (ethical trade audit). We are also BSCI-compliant and have passed SGS third-party factory audits for social compliance, product safety, and environmental management. All certifications are renewed through annual surveillance audits by SGS, Bureau Veritas, or SAI Global. Certificates are available for client review during the onboarding process. We are a Diamond Member on Made-in-China.com (since 2026) with on-site factory verification.
