FAQ

Q: What is the difference between embossing and debossing, and which is more durable? A: Embossing raises the design above the surface, creating a 3D tactile effect. Debossing presses the design into the surface. Both are equally durable on rigid boxes. Embossing is more visually dramatic and catches light better, making it the preferred choice for luxury corporate packaging. We can produce embossing depths from subtle 0.5mm blind emboss to dramatic 2.5mm deep relief. The embossing plate is made of brass for production runs exceeding 5,000 units to maintain crisp edges throughout the run.

Q: Can the satin ribbon be customized with printed text or a pattern? A: Yes — we offer custom-printed satin ribbon with your brand name, tagline, or pattern in up to 3 colors. Ribbon printing requires a minimum order of 2,000 meters, which typically covers 3,000–5,000 boxes depending on box size. Standard solid-color ribbon in any Pantone shade has no minimum beyond the box MOQ. Ribbon ends can be finished with heat-sealed edges, V-cut tails, or metallic aglets.

Q: Is this box suitable for shipping products directly to customers, or is it for hand-delivery only? A: This rigid presentation box is designed to be the primary packaging that sits inside a shipping carton. For direct-to-consumer shipping, we recommend adding a fitted corrugated outer mailer that protects the rigid box during transit. We can supply the complete packaging system — rigid inner box + custom corrugated outer mailer — as a coordinated set. Alternatively, the rigid box alone is suitable for hand-delivery and in-store presentation.

Q: What is the lead time for a fully custom embossed rigid box? A: Standard lead time is 15–20 business days from artwork approval: 3 days for embossing die fabrication, 5–7 days for pre-production samples, and 7–10 days for mass production after sample approval. Rush production (10 business days) is available for an additional 20% surcharge, subject to production schedule availability.
