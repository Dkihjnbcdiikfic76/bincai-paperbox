FAQ

Q1: What is the MOQ for custom corrugated shipping boxes? MOQ is 500 pieces for standard sizes with flexo printing. For full CMYK litho-laminate or complex die-cut structures, MOQ is 1,000 pieces. Trial orders below MOQ are available at a small setup surcharge. Free pre-production samples ship in 5–7 business days (freight collect).

Q2: How do I choose between B-flute, E-flute, and double-wall? B-flute (3 mm) is the workhorse for general e-commerce shipping — good cushioning and stacking strength. E-flute (1.5 mm) offers a smoother printing surface ideal for premium branded mailers and retail-ready packaging. BC double-wall (6 mm) delivers maximum crush resistance for heavy items (10+ kg) or fragile products like glassware and electronics requiring ISTA 1A transit protection.

Q3: What is the difference between double-wall and single-wall corrugated? Single-wall (one flute layer) is lighter and more cost-effective for items under 10 kg. Double-wall (two flute layers, e.g., BC or BE) roughly doubles edge crush strength and burst resistance, making it the right choice for heavy, fragile, or high-value shipments. Double-wall also performs significantly better in stacked pallet storage.

Q4: How much does flat-pack shipping save? Flat-pack (knocked-down) corrugated boxes reduce shipping volume by up to 70% compared to pre-erected boxes. A standard 40-ft container holds approximately 25,000–40,000 flat-packed mailer boxes depending on size. This dramatically lowers per-unit ocean freight cost and reduces carbon emissions per shipment.

Q5: What is the production lead time? Standard lead time is 15–20 business days after artwork approval and deposit — including BHS corrugator run, printing, die-cutting, gluing, and QC. Rush orders can be completed in 10–12 days with advance scheduling. Repeat orders with no artwork changes ship in 10–15 days.
