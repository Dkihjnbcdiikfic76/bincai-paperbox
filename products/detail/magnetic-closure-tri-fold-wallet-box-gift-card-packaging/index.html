FAQ

Q: How do you prevent the magnets from repelling each other — I’ve had boxes where the lid won’t stay closed because the magnets were installed backward?

A: This is a known failure point in low-cost magnetic box production, and we’ve engineered it out of our process. Every magnet pair goes through a 100% automated polarity verification at the insertion station — an optical sensor checks north/south orientation before the magnet is pressed into the board cavity. This happens in real-time on the assembly line; any misoriented magnet is rejected and replaced before the box proceeds. Additionally, each completed box undergoes a closure test where the operator verifies the magnetic snap — this is a quick tactile check that takes under 1 second per box. Our process achieves zero repelling-magnet rejects. For extra assurance, we perform a 50-unit batch pull test every shift with a digital force gauge, verifying 4–6N closure force per magnet pair.

Q: Will the soft-touch finish show fingerprints after handling — especially in retail environments where customers open and close the box repeatedly?

A: The soft-touch BOPP 18-micron matte lamination we use is specifically formulated to be fingerprint-resistant — unlike cheaper soft-touch coatings that attract oils and show every touch. In our Sutherland Rub Testing (100 cycles, equivalent to hundreds of retail handling interactions), the surface shows no visible wear or fingerprint accumulation. For high-traffic retail environments (department store gift card displays, hotel front desk), we recommend: (a) the matte lamination in darker colors (black, navy, deep burgundy) which are naturally more forgiving, or (b) an optional anti-microbial over-coat that also provides enhanced fingerprint resistance. Both options are available with no MOQ increase.

Q: Can we fit multiple gift cards in one box — for example, a set of brand partner cards or a “choose your experience” multi-voucher set?

A: Yes — we offer several multi-card configurations. The simplest is a single die-cut slot sized for 2–3 cards stacked (standard CR80 thickness is 0.76mm each, so 3 cards = 2.28mm total). For premium multi-card presentations, we offer: (a) multiple individual slots in a grid layout (up to 4 slots on a quad-fold panel), (b) an accordion-style interior with dedicated slots for each card, (c) elastic ribbon bands holding cards in a cascading offset arrangement (each card partially visible behind the previous), or (d) a gate-fold design where two interior panels each hold 1–2 cards. The gate-fold is particularly effective for “his and hers” or “choose your experience” concepts. Multi-card configurations typically require MOQ 1,000 units.

Q: Can we embed an NFC tag or QR code into the box for digital activation or augmented reality experiences?

A: Yes — both are standard options. For NFC: we embed a passive NFC tag (NTAG213 or NTAG216 — 13.56 MHz, ISO 14443-A compliant) between the board layers during assembly. The tag is completely invisible from the exterior and interior, and we verify read functionality on 100% of units. The recipient taps their phone to the box to activate the gift card, view a personalized video message, or launch an AR experience. For QR codes: we print a unique QR code directly on the interior panel using our Heidelberg offset press with photographic-quality reproduction. Variable data QR codes (unique codes per box) are printed via digital inkjet on the assembly line. Both NFC and QR code options add approximately $0.15–0.30 per unit and are available at MOQ 500.

Q: What is the difference between the tri-fold and the bi-fold design — and when would I choose one over the other?

A: The tri-fold design (3 panels) provides more real estate — typically a cover panel + two interior panels. This allows for: brand story or instructions on the left interior panel, and the gift card/certificate on the right interior panel. The tri-fold is ideal when you want to include a printed message, terms and conditions, or brand narrative alongside the gift card. The bi-fold design (2 panels) is more compact and economical — cover panel + single interior panel. This is best when the card itself carries all the necessary information and the packaging is purely for presentation. The bi-fold typically costs 15–20% less due to reduced material and simpler die-cutting. Both formats deliver the same premium magnetic snap experience. We also offer the quad-fold (4-panel) for corporate gift sets that include multiple items — card + certificate + envelope pocket + brand message panel.
