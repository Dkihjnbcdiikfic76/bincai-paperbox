FAQ

Q: How is this box different from regular “biodegradable” packaging claims? A: Unlike unregulated “biodegradable” claims, our compostable chocolate box carries third-party certifications: EN 13432 (European standard, certified by DIN CERTCO) and ASTM D6400 (US standard, certified by BPI). These certifications require the entire product — paperboard, PLA window, inks, and adhesive — to disintegrate by 90% within 12 weeks and biodegrade by 90% within 6 months in industrial composting conditions at 58±2°C. We provide certification documentation with every order.

Q: Does the PLA window get cloudy or brittle over time? A: Our PLA window film is stabilized for packaging applications with a service life of 12–18 months at room temperature (20–25°C) and normal humidity. It remains optically clear throughout the product’s shelf life. PLA only begins to hydrolyze (break down) when exposed to sustained temperatures above 58°C and high humidity — conditions found only in industrial composting facilities, not in normal retail or consumer environments.

Q: Can food-safe requirements be met for direct chocolate contact? A: Yes. The kraft board is manufactured without recycled content in the food-contact layer, soy-based inks are formulated to EU Directive 2002/72/EC for food contact materials, and the PLA window is FDA-compliant under 21 CFR 177.1500. We recommend a food-grade PE or PLA barrier coating for unwrapped chocolates. For individually wrapped chocolates or truffles in paper cups, the standard configuration is fully compliant.
