Seven-Stage QC Gates

  1. Paper Roll Inspection — Burst strength (≥200 PSI), ECT (≥32 lbs/in), moisture content 6–8%, caliper verification per TAPPI T-411
  2. Corrugator Monitoring — BHS 2.5m inline flute height, bond strength (PAT pin adhesion test), caliper — all digitally recorded per reel
  3. Pre-Press & Plate — Flexo plate inspection for wear, dot gain calibration, and registration marks verified on proof pull before production run
  4. Print Run — Inline spectrophotometer check every 500 sheets; color density per ISO 12647-2; EN 71-3 compliance spot-testing for toy safety
  5. Die-Cutting & Window — First-off approval for window cut precision ±0.5mm; window film adhesion peel test per ASTM D5264; anti-fog/anti-scratch verification
  6. Assembly — 100% window clarity inspection; peg-hole alignment; structural integrity — fold and lock verification on random samples
  7. Final Audit — AQL 2.5 Level II per ISO 2859-1; ISTA 1A drop test (10 drops, 760mm) on every batch; ECT re-verification; EN 71-3 heavy metals certificate per order

FAQ

Q: Are the inks truly safe for children’s toys — what testing backs this up?

A: Yes — all inks used on our toy packaging boxes are soy-based with heavy-metal-free pigments, tested to EN 71-3 (Migration of Certain Elements) for lead, cadmium, mercury, and chromium — all below 1 ppm detection limits. We also test to US CPSIA Section 101 for lead in surface coatings (< 90 ppm). Every production batch includes an EN 71-3 compliance certificate. For toys intended for children under 3 years, we offer an additional saliva-simulant migration test (EN 71-1). Our inks are low-VOC, solvent-free, and printed with water-based aqueous coating — no solvent odor that could transfer to the toy inside.

Q: Can the window film be made from biodegradable or recycled material?

A: Yes — we offer three window film options: (1) rPET (30% post-consumer recycled) with anti-fog coating, (2) PLA (polylactic acid) biodegradable film certified to EN 13432 industrial composting standard — clarity >88% light transmission, and (3) FSC-certified cellulose acetate film (wood-pulp based, home-compostable). The PLA option adds approximately 15–20% to material cost; cellulose acetate adds 25–30%. For brands that prioritize full recyclability, we recommend the windowless option with a printed illustration of the toy on the box exterior — achieving 100% curbside recyclable corrugated without any plastic film separation requirement.

Q: What’s the minimum order for a custom die-cut window shape — and how long does tooling take?

A: Standard rectangular or circular windows require no custom tooling and carry an MOQ of 500 units. Custom die-cut window shapes (star, heart, character silhouette, brand logo outline) require a custom steel-rule die with an MOQ of 1,000 units. Die fabrication takes 3–5 working days and costs $80–150 depending on complexity. The die is yours to keep and can be reused on future orders at no additional cost. We recommend providing vector artwork (AI/EPS/PDF) for the window shape for fastest turnaround.

Q: Can you print CMYK on both sides of the corrugated — inside and outside?

A: Yes — we offer double-sided CMYK offset printing. The outside is printed on white-top kraft liner (coated one-side) for vibrant retail graphics; the inside is printed on natural kraft liner in single-color or simplified CMYK for an enhanced unboxing experience (brand story, assembly instructions, care guide, or collector artwork). The inside print adds approximately 10–15% to unit cost and requires an additional printing pass. This is particularly effective for premium toy brands where the box itself becomes part of the play experience — think LEGO, board games, or collectible figurines.
