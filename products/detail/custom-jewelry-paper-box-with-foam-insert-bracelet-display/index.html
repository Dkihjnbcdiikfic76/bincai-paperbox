Quality Control — 15 Checkpoints

Every jewelry box passes through this QC gate system:

  1. Material Inspection — Greyboard caliper ±0.1mm, paper GSM verification, velvet pile height check
  2. Pre-Press Proof — Color bars, registration marks, foil alignment verification
  3. Print Color — ΔE ≤ 2.0 spectrophotometer reading against approved proof (ISO 12647-2)
  4. Foil Adhesion — Tape test per ASTM D3359, zero peel on all foil areas
  5. Emboss Registration — ±0.2mm alignment to printed artwork; multi-level depth verified with dial indicator
  6. Lamination Bond — ≥2.5 N/cm² peel strength, zero bubbling or edge lift
  7. Board Cutting — Dimensional tolerance ±0.3mm on all panels
  8. Grooving/Scoring — Channel depth within 0.1mm of target; no crushed flutes
  9. Wrap-Up — Tight wrap with zero wrinkles; mitered corners ±0.5mm
  10. Magnet Installation — Neodymium magnets flush-mounted; hold strength ≥3N
  11. Insert Fit — Foam cavity ±0.3mm to product dimensions; friction-fit verified
  12. Lining Adhesion — Velvet/fabric peel test ≥2.0 N/cm²; zero loose edges
  13. Final Assembly — Lid alignment ±0.5mm; closure tension consistent across batch
  14. Cosmetic Audit — 100% visual under 2000 lux lighting; zero surface defects
  15. Pack-Out — Individual polybag + bubble wrap; master carton drop-tested per ISTA 1A

FAQ

Q: What foam density and material options are available for the jewelry insert? A: We offer die-cut EVA foam in densities from 1mm to 6mm, available in black, white, grey, or custom Pantone-matched colors. For premium applications, we provide velvet-flocked vacuum-formed trays that cradle each piece precisely. All foam cavities are CNC-cut on our Bobst SP 102 BMA to ±0.3mm tolerance, ensuring a friction-fit hold for rings, bracelets, necklaces, and earrings without movement during transit.

Q: What is the minimum order quantity and sampling process? A: Standard MOQ is 500 units per design. We provide free pre-production samples including structural mockup with actual materials (velvet, foam, magnets) within 5-7 working days. Digital 3D renderings are available within 48 hours. Trial orders of 200 units are available at a premium for photo shoots and market testing.

Q: How does Bincai ensure consistent color across large jewelry box orders? A: Our KBA Rapida 105 4-color press runs at 15,000 sheets/hour with inline spectrophotometer monitoring. Color consistency is maintained within ΔE ≤ 2.0 across the entire run per ISO 12647-2. The Heidelberg Speedmaster CD 102 7+1 UV adds inline coating in the same pass, eliminating registration drift between color and finish layers.

Q: What certifications does Bincai hold for sustainable jewelry packaging? Our 350+ skilled workers operate across the 18,000 sqm facility under documented quality systems. All greyboard and art paper sourcing is fully traceable.
