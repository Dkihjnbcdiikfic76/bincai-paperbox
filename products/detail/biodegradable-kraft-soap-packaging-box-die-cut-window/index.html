FAQ

Q: Is the PLA window film truly compostable, or does it require industrial facilities?

A: The PLA film used in our window boxes is certified to both EN 13432 (European standard) and ASTM D6400 (US standard) for industrial composting. Under industrial composting conditions (58°C ±2°C, controlled humidity), the film biodegrades ≥90% within 180 days per ASTM D5338 testing. While home composting is slower (typically 12–24 months depending on conditions), the film is made from renewable plant starch (corn or sugarcane) rather than petroleum. We provide full certification documentation with every shipment for your sustainability claims.

Q: Does the PLA window affect the shelf life or appearance of the soap inside?

A: No. PLA film has excellent clarity (90%+ light transmission) comparable to PET, ensuring your soap’s colors and textures are fully visible. It has moderate moisture vapor transmission, which is actually beneficial for cold-process soaps that continue to cure slightly after packaging. For soaps with high essential oil content or hygroscopic ingredients, we can add a micro-perforation pattern to the PLA film for controlled breathability. The film is food-contact safe and does not impart any odor or taste to the product.

Q: Can the box be printed with my brand colors using soy-based inks?

A: Yes. Soy-based inks support full CMYK color gamut on kraft paperboard. While the natural brown kraft background shifts colors slightly warmer (blues appear slightly muted, earth tones appear richer), many eco-brands embrace this aesthetic as part of their natural brand identity. For brighter, more color-accurate printing, we recommend white kraft paperboard — it provides a neutral white base while maintaining the same FSC certification and compostability. We can provide printed samples on both substrates so you can compare before committing to production.

Q: What’s the difference between this box and a standard PET window box?

A: Three critical differences: (1) Material — our window uses PLA (plant-based, compostable) instead of PET (petroleum-based, 500+ year decomposition); (2) End-of-life — the entire box is biodegradable and compostable, whereas PET window boxes must have the window manually removed before recycling (most consumers don’t do this, sending the whole box to landfill); (3) Marketing value — the “100% compostable” claim on your packaging has proven to increase purchase intent by 30–50% among eco-conscious consumers according to multiple consumer studies. The per-unit cost premium for PLA vs. PET is approximately 10–15%, making it an affordable sustainability upgrade.
