FAQ

Q: How does the grease-resistant coating work, and will it prevent oil stains on the outside of the box? A: Our grease-resistant aqueous coating is a water-based fluorochemical treatment applied to the interior surface of the paperboard during the coating process. It creates a molecular barrier that repels oil and grease while remaining fully recyclable and compostable (unlike PE or wax coatings). In standardized Kit testing (TAPPI T559), our coating achieves Kit values of 8–12, meaning it withstands aggressive oils like butter, lard, and vegetable shortening without any bleed-through to the exterior. For extra-heavy applications like deep-fried foods, we can upgrade to a double-coating with Kit 12+ performance.

Q: What’s the difference between crash-lock bottom and auto-bottom — or are they the same thing? A: They are the same mechanism — both terms describe a folding carton bottom that uses interlocking die-cut tabs and precision-scored crease lines that automatically lock into position when the carton is squared up. “Crash-lock” is the traditional industry term (referring to the speed of assembly), while “auto-bottom” is the more modern, descriptive term. The mechanism is identical: no adhesive, no tape, no manual folding — the bottom locks itself under tension in under a second. These cartons ship flat (knocked down) for maximum transport efficiency.

Q: Are these cartons certified for direct food contact? A: Yes, our food-grade configuration uses water-based adhesives, low-migration offset inks, and FDA-compliant aqueous coatings suitable for direct food contact with dry and fatty foodstuffs. We provide migration testing certificates showing compliance with FDA 21 CFR 176.170 (paper and paperboard in contact with aqueous and fatty foods) and EU Framework Regulation (EC) No 1935/2004. For unwrapped baked goods, we recommend the grease-resistant coating plus an optional PE or PLA barrier film for extended shelf-life protection.

Sustainability

Bincai is committed to environmentally responsible packaging. Our folding cartons support your brand’s sustainability goals without compromising on quality or shelf impact:

  • FSC Certified Paper — All paperboard used in our folding cartons is sourced from FSC-certified forests and controlled sources. Forest Stewardship Council certification ensures that the fiber in your packaging comes from responsibly managed forests that protect biodiversity, worker rights, and indigenous communities.

  • Fully Recyclable — Unlike PE-laminated or wax-coated alternatives, our grease-resistant aqueous coating does not interfere with paper recycling. Cartons can be recycled curbside in standard paper waste streams without separation, supporting circular economy goals and retailer packaging mandates.

  • Compostable Option — For bakeries targeting zero-waste operations, we offer a compostable configuration using PLA-coated board and soy-based inks. This configuration meets EN 13432 industrial composting standards and is suitable for food-waste co-collection programs.

  • Soy-Based Inks — We offer soy-based and vegetable-based offset inks as an alternative to conventional petroleum-based inks. Soy inks produce vibrant color reproduction while emitting fewer VOCs during printing and making the printed carton easier to de-ink during recycling.

  • Flat-Ship Efficiency — Crash-lock bottom cartons ship flat (knocked down), maximizing pallet density and dramatically reducing carbon footprint per unit. A pallet carrying 3,000–8,000 flat cartons replaces the equivalent of 10+ pallets of pre-assembled boxes — cutting transport emissions, fuel consumption, and your inbound freight costs by up to 85%.
