FAQ

Q: What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for this silver book-shaped cosmetic box?

A: Standard MOQ is 500–1,000 units per design, depending on box size and finishing complexity. For prototype or market-testing runs, Bincai offers flexible low-volume production starting from 300 units at a modest per-unit premium. We work with startups and established brands alike — talk to our sales team about your specific launch timeline and budget.

Q: Can I choose a different velvet color for the interior insert?

A: Absolutely. The blue velvet shown is one of 100+ Pantone-matched flocking colors available. Choose from deep black, royal purple, champagne, rose pink, emerald green, or any custom shade you need. The electrostatic flocking process achieves uniform 0.5mm pile height and rich, consistent color regardless of your selection. You can even specify a different color for the insert tray vs. the inner lid lining.

Q: How many open/close cycles can the magnetic closure withstand?

A: The book-style magnetic side flap is engineered for long-term durability. Each box uses 2–4 N35-grade neodymium magnets calibrated to 3.5–5.5N pull-force per pair. Our in-house testing protocol runs 500+ open/close cycles with zero magnet dislodgement, zero hinge paper tear, and no measurable loss of magnetic hold strength. This exceeds the lifespan of a typical luxury cosmetic gift set by a wide margin.

Q: What is the typical lead time for a custom order?

A: Standard production lead time is 15–25 business days after sample approval and deposit confirmation, depending on order volume and finishing complexity. Rush orders can be accommodated at 10–12 business days. Factors affecting lead time include custom die fabrication (adds 3–5 days for new insert dies), hot-foil stamping die production, and special material sourcing. Sea freight from our Foshan, China factory to major ports worldwide typically adds 15–30 days depending on destination.

Q: Is there a minimum order requirement for custom die-cut insert tooling?

A: Yes, custom die-cut EVA foam or cardboard insert trays require a one-time tooling investment. The die fabrication cost ranges from $80–$250 depending on cavity count and complexity. This is a flat, non-recurring charge — the die is retained on file for all future reorders at no additional tooling cost. For standard rectangular cavities (e.g., a simple bottle + jar layout), die costs are at the lower end of this range.

Q: Do you offer samples, and how does the sampling process work?

A: Yes. Bincai offers two sample tracks: (1) Stock Sample — an existing book-shaped box from our showroom with similar dimensions and finishing, shipped within 2–3 business days for freight cost only (refundable against your first order). (2) Custom Pre-Production Sample — built to your exact specifications with your artwork, chosen materials, and custom die-cut insert. Typical turnaround is 7–10 business days at $80–$200 per sample (credited against your production order). 3D structural design files and digital proofs are provided before physical sampling begins.
