Product Overview

The Bincai Die-Cut Corrugated Candle Shipping Mailer solves the #1 pain point for candle brands: jars arriving cracked. An integrated EPE foam cradle, precision die-cut to fit your specific jar dimensions, suspends the candle away from all six walls of the box — the same protective principle used in electronics packaging, adapted for heavy glass. The self-locking bottom assembles in under 5 seconds without tape or adhesive. Full-color flexo printing on the exterior turns a shipping box into a brand billboard. Seven-Stage QC Gates

  1. Incoming Roll Stock — basis weight verification, moisture content (6-8% target), ECT pre-test on combined board samples
  2. Corrugator Run — flute formation check, bond strength (pin adhesion TAPPI T-821), caliper measurement every 1,000 linear meters
  3. Flexo Printing — color-to-color registration check, ink adhesion tape test, 100% visual every 500 sheets
  4. Die-Cutting — dimensional verification ±1.5mm, crease depth check for self-locking fold lines
  5. Foam Cradle Fit — go/no-go gauge: jar must seat fully in cradle with no lateral movement > 2mm
  6. Finished Goods Audit — AQL 4.0 (major), AQL 2.5 (minor) per ANSI/ASQ Z1.4
  7. Pre-Shipment Inspection — random sample pull, ISTA 1A drop test on 3 random units, full assembly and disassembly functional test

FAQ

Q: What jar weights can this mailer protect?

A: The standard B-flute configuration protects candle jars up to 500g through ISTA 1A drop testing (10 drops from 760mm). For jars weighing 500g-1.0kg, we recommend C-flute (4mm) for the box structure. For heavy multi-wick jars above 1.0kg, upgrade to BC double-wall board and increase foam cradle thickness to 30mm. We’ll specify the right board grade based on your jar weight during sampling — no guesswork.

Q: How is the self-locking bottom different from a taped bottom?

A: The self-locking bottom uses pre-scored fold lines that interlock when folded in sequence — no tape, adhesive, or tools required. Assembly takes under 5 seconds per box. The locking tabs are engineered to hold under the full weight of the candle jar during transit. The key advantage for candle brands: customers can easily fold the box flat for recycling without fighting tape or staples.

Q: Can the foam cradle be replaced with a more sustainable material?

A: Yes. We offer molded pulp cradles (100% recycled paper, home-compostable) as an alternative to EPE foam. Molded pulp provides comparable cushioning for jars up to 300g but has slightly lower shock absorption for heavier jars. The trade-off: molded pulp cradles add approximately 20% to the unit cost and require minimum order quantities of 2,000 units due to mold tooling. For brands prioritizing sustainability over maximum protection, molded pulp is our recommended path.

Q: Will the flexo print hold up during transit — won’t it scuff?

A: Our water-based flexo inks are formulated for abrasion resistance. Under normal single-trip shipping conditions (factory → warehouse → customer), scuffing is minimal. For candle subscription boxes that ship monthly and are handled extensively, we recommend adding a matte aqueous coating — it adds approximately 6% to unit cost but provides scuff resistance comparable to offset-printed folding cartons.

Q: What’s the minimum order for a fully custom die-cut shape?

A: MOQ is 500 units for a custom die-cut mailer with new cutting and creasing dies. The steel rule die is produced in-house in 3-5 days and included at no charge for orders of 2,000+ units. For smaller orders, the die charge is $150-250 depending on complexity. We can also adapt your jar dimensions to an existing mailer template from our die library — if a close match exists, MOQ is 500 units with no die charge.
