FAQ

Q: Are the metal corners purely decorative or do they provide structural protection?

A: The metal corners serve dual purpose — they are both decorative and structural. Each corner is die-cast from zinc alloy (2.0–3.0mm thickness), electroplated for finish durability, and mechanically riveted through the full thickness of the greyboard. This means the corners protect the most vulnerable impact points of the box during shipping and handling, preventing corner crush and board delamination. Our ISTA 3A drop testing confirms that riveted corners maintain box integrity through 1.2m drops onto all faces and corners. For purely decorative applications, we offer surface-mounted corners, but we recommend riveted for any box that will be shipped or handled frequently.

Q: What leather grades and colors are available for the handle? Can we match our brand’s specific color?

A: Our standard offering is full-grain vegetable-tanned bovine leather (2.5–3.0mm thickness) in natural brown, dark brown, or black. Full-grain leather retains the natural grain pattern and develops a patina over time — ideal for luxury brands that value authenticity. For brands requiring specific Pantone-matched colors, we offer PU leather (polyurethane-coated split leather) in any PMS color with a minimum order of 1,000 units. The handle is attached via reinforced brass tubular rivets (6mm shank diameter) with domed or flat head profiles. We can also source exotic leathers (crocodile-embossed, ostrich-embossed) for limited editions — MOQ and lead time vary by material.

Q: Can we have a multi-tier interior — for example, a watch on top and accessories below?

A: Yes — multi-tier trunk interiors are one of our specialties. The most common configuration is a two-tier design: the upper tray (removable) holds the primary product (watch, bottle, jewelry) while the lower compartment provides storage for accessories, certificates, care cards, or additional products. We construct this using precision die-cut EVA foam (laser-cut to product silhouette), cardstock partitions wrapped in velvet, or molded pulp trays. The upper tray typically includes a satin ribbon pull-tab for easy lifting. Three-tier configurations are available for larger trunks (300×200mm footprint minimum). All tiers are engineered to stack securely without shifting during transit.

Q: What certifications apply to the metal hardware and leather components?

A: For metal hardware (corners, rivets, latches): REACH (EC 1907/2006) compliance documentation is standard — all zinc alloy castings and electroplated finishes are tested for restricted substances including lead, cadmium, mercury, and hexavalent chromium. For the US market, CPSIA (Consumer Product Safety Improvement Act) compliance certificates are available, confirming total lead content ≤ 100 ppm in surface coatings and ≤ 100 ppm in substrates. For leather: REACH compliance for restricted azo dyes and chromium VI content. All hardware and leather suppliers maintain ISO 9001 certification, and we maintain full batch traceability from raw material lot number to finished box.

Q: What is the minimum order quantity for a fully custom trunk box with new tooling?

A: MOQ is 500 units for boxes using our existing die-library sizes and standard hardware. For a fully custom trunk box requiring new cutting dies, custom corner tooling (if non-standard profile or size), and custom leather handle color, the MOQ is 1,000 units. New die-board production takes 24 hours (in-house CNC), custom corner mold fabrication takes 5–7 days (external foundry), and physical samples are delivered within 7 days of die approval. The total sampling-to-production timeline is typically 14–21 days for a fully custom trunk.

Format Selection: Trunk vs Book-Style vs Drawer-Slide

Trunk boxes occupy the premium tier of rigid packaging. Here’s when each format is the right choice:

Decision rule: Choose trunk when: (a) the product weighs over 2kg (spirits bottles, multi-tool sets), (b) the box itself is intended to be kept and displayed (collector’s editions, heirloom gifts), or (c) metal hardware adds authenticity (vintage/steampunk/industrial aesthetics). Choose book-style for lightweight, flat products where the opening motion mimics a book reveal. Choose drawer-slide for cosmetics and jewelry where the sliding action reveals product in stages.
