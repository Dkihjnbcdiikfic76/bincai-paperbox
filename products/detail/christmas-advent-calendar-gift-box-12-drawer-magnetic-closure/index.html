FAQ

Q: Can you make a 24-drawer version for a full advent calendar?

A: Yes, 24-drawer is our second most popular configuration after 12-drawer. We can also produce 31-drawer (full December countdown) or custom counts (5, 8, 15 drawers). Each drawer count requires a proportional adjustment to the outer case dimensions — our design team provides 3D renderings before production for your approval.

Q: Are the drawers food-safe for chocolate and candy?

A: Yes. All drawer interiors are coated with a water-based aqueous coating that complies with FDA 21 CFR 176.170 for dry food contact. For products with higher fat content (truffles, oil-based confections), we offer an optional grease-resistant barrier coating. Full migration testing documentation is available with every production batch.

Q: What’s the difference between an advent calendar box and a regular drawer box?

A: Advent calendar boxes are designed specifically for sequential daily opening. Key features include: individually numbered drawers (1–12, 1–24, or 1–31), front-facing pull tabs for easy one-handed drawer access, and a magnetic or ribbon closure that maintains structural integrity through repeated daily use over 3–4 weeks. The drawer mechanism is tested for 100+ open/close cycles — far beyond the 12–31 uses during the advent period.

Q: Can the drawer numbering be foil-stamped in different colors?

A: Absolutely. Standard options include gold, silver, rose gold, copper, and holographic foil. We can also apply different foil colors per drawer — for example, red foil for weekend drawers and gold for weekday drawers — creating an engaging color-coded countdown experience. Foil registration is maintained within ±0.15mm for crisp, clean numbering.

Q: How are the drawers secured during shipping — won’t they slide out?

A: Each drawer fits snugly within its housing with a calibrated friction fit (0.3–0.5mm clearance). The magnetic front flap closure adds an additional retention layer. For extra security during long-distance shipping, we offer an optional tear-away retention band or shrink-wrap sleeve that the end customer removes before the first use, ensuring all 12 drawers arrive perfectly in place."
