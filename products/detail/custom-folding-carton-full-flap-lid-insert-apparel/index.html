Product Overview

Bincai’s full-flap folding carton delivers a premium department-store unboxing experience for luxury apparel brands — at folding carton economics. The oversized hinged lid opens flat to 180°, transforming the box into a product presentation stage. Inside, a precision die-cut insert tray cradles folded garments, scarves, neckties, or accessories with zero movement during transit. The exterior combines soft-touch matte lamination (3-5μm velvet-like coating) with selective spot UV gloss accents on brand logos — the tactile contrast between matte and gloss creates an immediate quality signal when the customer picks up the box.

The auto-bottom locking base assembles in under 3 seconds — no glue, no tape, no frustration. Flat-shipped design: 10× more units per pallet than equivalent rigid boxes, translating to 60-70% ocean freight savings. Manufactured on KBA 1050 4-color offset and Heidelberg 7+1 UV presses in our 18,000 sqm Foshan facility. 23 years of folding carton engineering expertise — Bincai has produced over 800 million folding cartons since 2003.

FAQ

Q: How does the soft-touch lamination hold up during shipping and handling? A: Soft-touch matte lamination is highly durable — Sutherland Rub Test validated at 50+ cycles with zero degradation. Unlike aqueous coatings that can scuff, the 3-5μm soft-touch film provides lasting tactile quality from factory floor to customer doorstep. For extra protection, we offer an anti-scuff over-lacquer that preserves the soft-touch feel through aggressive fulfillment center handling.

Q: Can the insert tray be designed for multiple garment types in the same box? A: Yes. We can die-cut a single insert tray with multiple compartments — for example, a shirt cavity (280×200mm), a tie channel (80×30mm), and cufflink slots (20×20mm each). The maximum recommended tray complexity is 6 compartments within 400×300mm. For more complex configurations, we suggest our two-tier or drawer-style boxes.

Q: What’s the minimum order for a fully custom die-cut insert tray? A: MOQ is 1,000 units for standard sizes with an existing insert design. Custom die-cut insert trays require a one-time die tooling fee ($150-300 depending on complexity) with a minimum production run of 2,000 units. The die is stored at our facility for future reorders at no additional tooling cost for 24 months.

Q: Can I get FSC certification on these boxes for my brand’s sustainability claims? A: Yes. Our FSC Chain-of-Custody certification (FSC Mix Credit) allows us to provide FSC-certified cartons with the FSC logo printed on the box. We can also provide soy-based inks and PLA (compostable) window film as eco-friendly upgrades. The FSC certification documentation is included with each shipment.

Q: What’s the lead time from order to delivery? A: Digital proof within 48 hours. Physical sample (actual board + print + soft-touch lamination) in 5-7 working days. Mass production: 10-14 working days after sample approval. Ocean freight adds 18-30 days to major ports; air freight 5-7 days for urgent orders. The flat-ship design also means faster fulfillment on your end — your team can assemble 200+ boxes per hour vs 50+ for rigid boxes.
