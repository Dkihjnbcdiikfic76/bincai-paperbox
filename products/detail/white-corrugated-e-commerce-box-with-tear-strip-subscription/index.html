FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for white corrugated e-commerce boxes? Our standard MOQ is 500 units for stock die-cut sizes with single-color print. For fully custom dimensions, multi-color CMYK offset printing, or specialty tear-strip configurations, the MOQ starts at 1,000 units. We offer volume discounts at 5,000, 10,000, and 50,000+ unit tiers. Contact our sales team for a detailed quote based on your exact specifications.

How do I choose the right flute type (E, B, BC, or F) for my product? Flute selection depends on product weight, fragility, and shipping conditions. E-flute (1.2mm) offers the best print surface for cosmetics, small electronics, and lightweight subscription items under 2kg. B-flute (2.5mm) provides superior crush resistance for books, apparel, and medium-weight items up to 5kg. BC-flute (6.0mm) is double-wall construction for heavy or fragile products over 5kg. F-flute (0.8mm) is a micro-flute ideal for luxury packaging where a smooth, rigid surface is essential. Our engineers can recommend the optimal flute profile after reviewing your product dimensions and transit requirements.

Are your boxes compliant with Amazon FBA packaging requirements? Yes. All our corrugated e-commerce boxes can be manufactured to Amazon FBA dimensional and weight specifications, including SIOC (Ships in Own Container) certification when required. We test to ISTA 6-Amazon.com-SIOC standards in our on-site lab, covering the full 10-drop sequence plus compression testing. We maintain a library of FBA-compliant die templates for common size tiers (Small Standard, Large Standard, Small Oversize, Large Oversize) to accelerate your go-to-market timeline.

How durable is the integrated tear strip — will it fail during shipping? Our tear strips are engineered for reliable consumer opening after transit. PET tear tape is laminated between the outer liner and fluting during corrugation, ensuring it cannot delaminate during handling. Initiation force is calibrated to ≤4.5N — easy for consumers but impossible to trigger accidentally. Every production run undergoes 100% tear-strip function testing at our QC station, and random samples from each lot are subjected to ISTA 1A drop testing to verify the strip remains intact after a full transit simulation.

How long does it take to receive pre-production samples? Pre-production samples are manufactured on our actual production equipment (not prototyping printers) and ship within 5–7 business days from artwork approval. Samples are free of charge for orders with confirmed intent. White-label samples from existing die templates can ship in 3 business days. We provide digital proofs within 24 hours and physical color-match proofs within 5 days for critical color-matching projects.
