Seven-Stage QC Gates

  1. Incoming Material Inspection — linerboard grammage, flute caliper, adhesive viscosity verification
  2. Corrugator QC — bond strength (pin adhesion test), warp control, moisture content at 6-8%
  3. Flexo/Litho Printing — color check every 500 sheets, ΔE ≤2.5 (flexo relaxed tolerance)
  4. Die-Cutting & Slotting — dimensional verification ±2mm, slot depth consistency
  5. Partition Fit Check — foam cavity fit to part, compression set test on EPE/EVA
  6. Finished Goods Audit — BCT compression test, AQL 2.5 (major), AQL 1.0 (minor) per ANSI/ASQ Z1.4
  7. Pre-Shipment Inspection — random sample pull, ISTA 3E distribution simulation on request

FAQ

Q: What is the minimum order quantity for custom industrial shippers?

A: MOQ is 500 units per design. For simpler RSC boxes without foam partitions, MOQ is 300 units. Sample turnaround is 5-7 days with free pre-production samples.

Q: Can the foam partitions be designed for specific part geometries?

A: Yes. Our in-house die-making team creates custom EPE/EVA foam tooling based on your 3D CAD files or physical part samples. Cavity tolerances are ±1mm. Foam densities from 20-80 kg/m³ are selected based on part weight and fragility.

Q: Can this box pass ISTA 3E distribution testing?

A: Yes. We offer ISTA 3E (unitized loads) and ISTA 3A (parcel delivery) testing through certified partner labs. The double-wall construction with custom foam partitioning is engineered for supply chain abuse — vibration, compression, and impact. Test reports included with first article inspection.

Q: What is the production lead time?

A: Standard lead time is 10-14 days after artwork and foam tooling approval. Rush orders can be expedited to 7 days. Foam tooling adds 3-5 days for first-time designs.

Q: Can the box be printed with full-color branding?

A: Yes. We offer litho-laminated offset printing (CMYK + spot) for premium brand presentation, 1-4 color flexo for cost-effective branding, and digital print for short runs. Litho-lam is printed on KBA 1050 press and laminated to corrugated board inline.

Competitive Differentiation — Double-Wall Corrugated Shipper vs Wood Crates vs Single-Wall Boxes

Industrial parts shipping is a high-stakes game where a single dropped pallet can mean ¥50,000 in damaged goods and a lost customer. The choice of shipping container directly impacts freight cost (weight × volume), damage claims (% of shipments), customs clearance (ISPM 15), and disposal fees (wood ≠ recyclable in cardboard stream).
