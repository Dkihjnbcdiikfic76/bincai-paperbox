FAQ

Q: What’s the difference between flexo and litho-laminated printing on corrugated? A: Flexographic printing (water-based, 1–4 colors) prints directly on the corrugated liner using flexible photopolymer plates — ideal for bold logos, simple graphics, and cost-effective branding. Litho-laminated printing first prints on coated white-top paper using Heidelberg 7+1 UV offset (CMYK + spot Pantone at 200 LPI), then laminates this pre-printed sheet to the corrugated medium. Litho-lamination delivers photo-quality graphics, fine text, gradients, and metallic/fluorescent inks — recommended when your branding includes product photography, complex illustrations, or premium aesthetic requirements. Litho-lam costs 30–50% more than flexo.

Q: Can the box be food-safe for direct food contact? A: Yes. For subscription boxes containing unpackaged food items (baked goods, fresh produce, confectionery), we configure the box with food-grade specifications: water-based flexo inks (FDA 21 CFR compliant for indirect food contact), starch-based adhesive (food-grade corn starch), and unbleached kraft liner with no recycled content in the food-contact layer. We provide migration testing documentation and FDA compliance certificates. For direct food contact (no inner packaging), we recommend adding a food-grade PE or PLA liner bag.

Q: How does the self-locking crash-lock bottom work? A: The crash-lock bottom uses pre-scored and pre-glued flaps that automatically interlock when the box is opened from its flat shipping state. No tape, no manual folding — just pull the sides apart and the bottom locks into place in under 3 seconds. The mechanism is tested to 5 kg load capacity (standard) and 10 kg (reinforced with additional glue points). This design reduces fulfillment center labor by approximately 40% compared to tape-bottom boxes, making it the preferred choice for high-volume subscription box operations.

Q: What’s the minimum order quantity per SKU? A: MOQ is 500 units for standard configurations (single-color flexo, B-flute kraft, standard sizes 200–400mm). Custom sizes (non-standard dimensions), multi-color flexo (3–4 colors), litho-laminated graphics, inside-print, or specialty flutes require 1,000–2,000 units per SKU. For subscription box brands with multiple monthly variants, we offer multi-SKU production runs where tooling costs are shared — contact our team for a multi-SKU quotation.

Q: Can I get different designs for the same box size (monthly theme variants)? A: Yes. This is our core subscription box model. Once the structural design and cutting die are approved, you can change the printing artwork for each monthly variant with no additional tooling cost — only plate charges for flexo or new printing plates for litho-lam. We support monthly, quarterly, or seasonal artwork rotations. Artwork files accepted in AI, PDF, or CDR format with outlined fonts and CMYK color space.

Q: What transit testing do the boxes undergo? A: Every box design is validated through ISTA 3A protocol before production release — this includes drop testing (10 drops from 760mm on corners, edges, and faces), random vibration testing (truck simulation for 60+ minutes), and compression testing (top-load to verify stacking strength during palletized shipping). We provide ISTA 3A certification documentation for your carrier compliance. Additional testing available: ISTA 6-Amazon.com (Amazon SIOC certification), temperature/humidity conditioning, and incline-impact testing.

Q: What’s the lead time for first-time orders vs. repeats? A: First-time orders: 10–15 working days (includes structural design, die fabrication, printing plate production, pre-production sample approval). Repeat orders with existing tooling: 7–10 working days. Rush service (5 working days) available with 15% surcharge for repeat orders. For subscription box launches with tight timelines, we offer split-shipment options — initial 20% air freight (3–5 days transit) for launch-day fulfillment, balance by sea freight (25–35 days).
