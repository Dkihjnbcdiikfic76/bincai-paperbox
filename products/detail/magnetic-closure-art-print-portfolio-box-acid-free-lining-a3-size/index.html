FAQ

Q: What does ‘acid-free’ mean, and why is it important for art prints? A: Acid-free paper has a pH of 7.0 or higher and is manufactured without acidic chemicals that cause paper to yellow, become brittle, and degrade over time. Standard paperboard contains lignin and acidic compounds that off-gas and can transfer to stored contents — this is why old newspapers turn yellow and fragile. Our portfolio boxes use ISO 9706-compliant acid-free lining paper (pH 7.5–8.5) and pH-neutral adhesives that will not cause image degradation, color shift, or embrittlement of your art prints even after decades of storage.

Q: Can the box accommodate different print quantities — say, from 5 to 50 prints? A: Yes. We offer three interior configurations: (1) Flat tray — holds 5–15 prints in a single stack, ideal for gallery sets; (2) Expansion gusset — accordion-fold side walls that expand to hold 20–50 prints with interleaving tissue; (3) Multi-tray — 2–4 removable trays each holding 10–15 prints, ideal for series or collections. Each configuration is custom-built to your specifications with the same acid-free materials throughout.

Q: Is the box sturdy enough for shipping art prints to customers? A: Yes. The 1,600gsm greyboard construction with reinforced corners and magnetic closure is designed to withstand commercial shipping. We test every portfolio box design through ISTA 3A simulated transit testing — including 10-drop test, compression test (200 lb top-load), and vibration test. For prints valued above $200, we recommend adding corner reinforcements and a corrugated outer shipping carton (available from Bincai as a matched set).

Sustainability

Bincai is committed to environmentally responsible packaging. Our archival portfolio boxes support your sustainability goals without compromising on preservation quality:

  • FSC Certified Paper — All paperboard and lining paper used in our portfolio boxes is sourced from FSC-certified forests and controlled sources. Forest Stewardship Council certification ensures that the fiber in your packaging comes from responsibly managed forests.

  • Recyclable Core Materials — The greyboard core of our boxes is made from 100% recycled post-consumer and post-industrial fiber. The acid-free paper lining, being uncoated and free of plastic lamination, is fully recyclable in standard paper waste streams.

  • Soy-Based Inks — We offer soy-based and vegetable-based offset inks as an alternative to conventional petroleum-based inks. Soy inks produce vibrant color reproduction while emitting fewer VOCs and making the printed surface easier to de-ink during recycling.

  • Plastic-Free Construction — The entire portfolio box — from greyboard core to paper wrap to magnetic closure — is plastic-free. There is no PET laminate, no PVC window, and no plastic insert. This makes the box fully compatible with paper recycling streams.

  • Long-Life Design — Unlike single-use packaging, our archival portfolio boxes are designed for decades of use. The acid-free materials, reinforced construction, and durable magnetic closure mean collectors and galleries can reuse these boxes for storage, inventory, and transport — reducing the need for replacement packaging over the artwork’s lifetime.
