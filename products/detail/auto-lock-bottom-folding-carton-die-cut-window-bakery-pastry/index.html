FAQ

Q: Does the auto-lock bottom really work reliably, or will cartons pop open during filling?

A: The auto-lock bottom is engineered for reliability. Our design uses 45° locking tab geometry with precisely calculated crease relief that creates positive mechanical interlock — once locked, the bottom cannot be pushed open from above. We 100% functionally test every production batch: each carton is auto-locked and load-tested at 150% of rated weight. Failure rate is <0.05%. For extra security with heavier products (>500g), we offer a reinforced auto-lock design with extended locking tabs.

Q: Are the inks and adhesives truly food-safe — can the carton touch food directly?

A: Our standard configuration is designed for indirect food contact — the carton holds food that is typically wrapped, bagged, or placed on a greaseproof liner. Our low-migration soy-based inks are tested to <10 ppb migration (EU 1935/2004). For direct food contact (e.g., unwrapped croissants placed directly in the carton), we offer FDA-compliant direct-contact configurations with an internal greaseproof barrier coating and certified direct-contact inks. Please specify your food-contact requirements when inquiring.

Q: Can you print inside the carton — for example, a surprise message or pattern when the customer opens it?

A: Yes. We offer single-side and double-side printing. Inside printing is commonly used for brand storytelling, social media handles, “thank you” messages, or surprise patterns that create a memorable unboxing moment. Inside printing adds approximately 15–20% to the print cost due to the additional pass through the press. The inside print uses the same food-safe low-migration inks as the exterior.

Q: What’s the minimum order quantity for a custom window shape?

A: MOQ is 1,000 units for standard window shapes (rectangle, square, circle, oval, arch). Custom die-cut window shapes (brand logo silhouette, product-shaped cutout, etc.) require a custom window cutting die at a one-time tooling cost of approximately $80–150, with MOQ of 2,000–3,000 units. The custom die is yours and we store it for future reorders at no additional charge.

Q: How flat do the cartons ship — do they take up a lot of warehouse space?

A: Cartons ship flat (unfolded) on pallets. A standard pallet (1200×1000mm) holds approximately 8,000–12,000 flat cartons depending on size and board thickness. This compact shipping format minimizes freight costs — ocean freight for 100,000 cartons typically fits on 8–12 pallets. The auto-lock bottom means your filling station operators simply pop them open with one squeeze — no assembly time required.
