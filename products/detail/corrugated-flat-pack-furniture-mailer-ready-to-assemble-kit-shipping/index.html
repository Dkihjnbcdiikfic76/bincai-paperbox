Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What flute should I choose for my furniture product weight? A: B-flute single-wall (3mm): lightweight items up to 5 kg (small shelves, children’s kits). BC double-wall (6mm): medium weight 5–15 kg (most RTA furniture — desks, chairs, cabinets). EB double-wall (8mm): heavy 15–25 kg (solid wood furniture, multi-panel kits). Triple-wall (12–15mm): premium/heavy 25+ kg. Our engineers recommend based on your product weight, dimensions, and shipping method.

Q: How do self-locking bottoms work and do they really eliminate tape? A: Yes. The crash-lock bottom is die-cut and scored so that when you fold the box, the bottom panels interlock automatically — no tape, no glue, no tools. It assembles in under 3 seconds and holds up to 25 kg for BC double-wall. Fulfillment centers report 30–40% packing time reduction compared to taped RSC boxes.

Q: Can you do full-color printing on corrugated boxes? A: Yes, two methods: (1) Litho-laminate — full CMYK offset printed on art paper then laminated to corrugated board (photographic quality, best for DTC brand presentation). (2) Pre-print liner — high-quality flexo or gravure printed on the outer liner before corrugation (cost-effective for large volumes). Single-color or 4-color direct flexo on kraft is also available for utility applications.

Q: What testing do you perform before shipping? A: Every new design undergoes ISTA 3A package performance testing in our in-house lab: 10-drop sequence from variable height, 60-minute random vibration per axis, and top-load compression. We also test ECT (edge crush), BCT (box compression), and burst strength per TAPPI standards. You receive the full test report with your pre-production sample.

Q: Can you add VCI (volatile corrosion inhibitor) protection for metal hardware? A: Yes. We can integrate VCI paper or VCI poly film into hardware compartments to prevent rust and corrosion on screws, bolts, Allen keys, and metal brackets during ocean freight and warehouse storage. VCI protection lasts 12–24 months depending on conditions.

Q: What’s the lead time for a new furniture mailer design? A: Structural design and 3D CAD: 2–3 days. Pre-production sample with ISTA 3A testing: 5–7 days. Production: 12–25 days depending on complexity (partitions, litho-laminate, printing). Rush service available at 8–12 days. We recommend starting 5–6 weeks before your target delivery date for first-time orders.

Q: Are your corrugated boxes recyclable and sustainable? A: Yes. Our boxes use water-based starch adhesive (solvent-free, biodegradable), kraft liners from FSC-certified sources, and soy-based flexo inks. The entire box — corrugated body, partitions, and kraft paper — is mono-material and 100% recyclable in standard paper/cardboard waste streams. We provide FSC Chain-of-Custody transaction certificates with every order.
