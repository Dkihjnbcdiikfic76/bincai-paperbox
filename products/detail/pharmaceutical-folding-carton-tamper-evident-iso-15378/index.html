FAQ

Q: What’s the difference between ISO 9001 and ISO 15378? A: ISO 9001 covers general quality management. ISO 15378:2017 is specifically for primary packaging materials for medicinal products — it adds CGMP requirements including contamination control, validated cleaning, personnel hygiene, controlled environment, risk management (ICH Q9), and full batch traceability. It’s the packaging equivalent of pharma GMP manufacturing.

Q: Can you print variable data (batch, expiry, serial number) on each carton? A: Yes — our Domino thermal inkjet system prints variable data inline at 600 dpi during folder-gluer operation. We support lot numbers, expiry dates, serial numbers, 2D DataMatrix codes, GS1-128 barcodes, and Pharmacode. Variable data is verified by camera inspection with reject diversion.

Q: Do you offer child-resistant (CR) folding cartons? A: Yes — we manufacture CR-certified designs including reverse-tuck with locking tab (CR-lok style) and push-and-turn insert cartons. Testing to ISO 8317 / 16 CFR 1700 is arranged through an independent certified lab. CR design adds approximately 15-25% to per-unit cost and requires 3,000+ MOQ.

Q: What’s your minimum order for Braille embossing? A: Braille embossing requires a dedicated embossing die (~300 USD one-time) and adds a post-print embossing step. MOQ is 2,000 units to amortize setup. Braille text must be provided by the customer per regulatory requirements; we can advise on correct Braille grade specification.
