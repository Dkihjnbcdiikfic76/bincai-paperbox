FAQ

Q: Is mushroom packaging strong enough to protect my product during shipping?

A: Yes — at 120-180 kg/m³ density, mycelium composite has compressive strength comparable to expanded polystyrene (EPS) foam, with the added benefit of being flexible rather than brittle (it deforms rather than shatters on impact). We conduct ISTA 3A drop and vibration testing for every product configuration. For fragile items like glass bottles or electronics, we recommend 16-20mm wall thickness and 160-180 kg/m³ density — our testing shows equivalent or better protection versus petroleum-based foam inserts.

Q: How long does the box last on the shelf before it starts to degrade?

A: Mycelium packaging is stable indefinitely under normal indoor storage conditions (20-25°C, 40-60% RH) — it will not degrade, sprout mushrooms, or attract pests. Degradation only begins when the box is placed in active composting conditions: soil contact, moisture, and microbial activity. For retail display and warehousing, the box behaves like any other rigid packaging material. The kraft outer wrap provides an additional moisture barrier for humid environments.

Q: Can Bincai grow the mycelium box to my exact product shape — for a truly custom-fit unboxing experience?

A: Yes — this is the unique advantage of grown-to-shape mycelium packaging. We create a negative mold of your product (from CAD, 3D scan, or physical sample), inoculate the mycelium-substrate mix into that mold, and the mycelium grows to precisely fit your product. The result is a nesting cavity that cradles your product without any additional insert or filler material. Mold tooling is CNC-machined from aluminum or HDPE and costs $500-2,000 depending on complexity. Lead time for new mold creation is 10-14 days.

Q: What certifications does the mushroom packaging carry for compostability claims?

A: Our mycelium packaging is certified to EN 13432 (European standard for industrially compostable packaging) and carries the TÜV Austria OK Compost HOME certification — the gold standard proving home compostability without industrial facilities. Additionally, the kraft outer wrap is FSC-certified, and the soy-based inks are certified for food-safe indirect contact. We also provide a lifecycle analysis (LCA) report showing the carbon-negative profile: each kg of mycelium packaging sequesters approximately 0.3 kg CO₂ equivalent during the growth phase.
