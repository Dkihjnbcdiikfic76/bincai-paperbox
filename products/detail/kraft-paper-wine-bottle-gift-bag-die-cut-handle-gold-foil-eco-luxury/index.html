Product Overview

Our kraft paper wine bottle gift bag marries eco-conscious materials with luxury presentation through the striking combination of natural kraft texture and gold foil stamping. Constructed from 250gsm FSC-certified kraft paper, the bag features a reinforced die-cut handle, square-bottom gusset, and is precision-sized for standard 750ml wine bottles. Gold foil hot stamping on the uncoated kraft surface creates an organic-luxury aesthetic — the warmth of kraft contrasting with metallic brilliance — that resonates with today’s sustainability-minded wine consumers. All printing uses soy-based inks (USDA BioPreferred certified) for complete biodegradability. Manufactured on our KBA 1050 4-color offset press at Bincai’s 18,000 sqm integrated factory. Every bag passes 7-stage QC from kraft grammage verification to final packaging audit. With 23 years of paper packaging expertise, we deliver wine bags that tell your brand’s story before the bottle is even revealed.

FAQ

Q: Will the gold foil adhere well to uncoated kraft paper? A: Yes! We use a specialized foil formulation for uncoated kraft that bites into the paper fibers rather than sitting on the surface. The result is excellent adhesion (≥95% in tape test) with a beautiful organic-metallic contrast.

Q: Can the wine bag support a full bottle by the handle alone? A: Absolutely. Our die-cut handle with internal reinforcement patch is tested to ≥15kg sustained load — a full 750ml wine bottle weighs approximately 1.2–1.5kg, well within the safety margin.

Q: Are the soy-based inks truly biodegradable? A: Yes! Our soy inks are USDA BioPreferred certified, VOC-free, and fully biodegradable. The entire bag — kraft paper, soy ink, and reinforcement patch — biodegrades in industrial composting conditions.

Q: What bottle shapes does this bag fit? A: The standard size fits Bordeaux (straight-sided), Burgundy (sloped shoulder), and Champagne bottles. For unusual shapes (Alsace flute, Rhône), we can adjust the gusset dimensions accordingly.

Q: Can you print on both sides of the bag? A: Yes! We can print on both exterior faces and even the gusset sides. This is popular for wineries that want brand messaging visible from multiple angles on retail shelves.

Q: What’s the difference between die-cut handles and rope handles? A: Die-cut handles are integrated into the bag body (no separate component) — more eco-friendly and cost-effective. Rope handles offer a more premium tactile feel. Both are available, with die-cut being the most popular for wine bags.
