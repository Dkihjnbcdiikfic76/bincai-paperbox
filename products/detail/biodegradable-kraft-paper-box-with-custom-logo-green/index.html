Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is the kraft paper box actually biodegradable? A: Yes. Our standard kraft boxes use unbleached kraft paperboard, soy-based inks, and water-based PVA adhesives. The complete box achieves ≥90% biodegradation within 180 days per EN 13432 and ASTM D6400 standards when processed in industrial composting facilities. We can provide third-party test certificates on request.

Q: Can I get 100% recycled kraft board? A: Absolutely. We offer 85–100% post-consumer recycled (PCR) kraft board options. The 100% PCR board has a slightly more textured surface than virgin kraft but prints beautifully with soy-based inks. FSC Recycled certification is available for full recycled-content chain-of-custody.

Q: What if I need a plastic window for product visibility? A: We offer PLA (polylactic acid) film windows — certified compostable per ASTM D6400 — as a plastic-free alternative to traditional PET/PVC windows. PLA windows add approximately $0.03–0.06/unit and have excellent clarity comparable to PET. For brands that can accept a PET window, we use recycled PET (rPET) options.

Q: How does the kraft box hold up in shipping? A: Kraft paperboard at 350–450gsm provides excellent structural integrity for e-commerce shipping. For heavier products or international freight, we recommend kraft-wrapped greyboard (1.5–2.5mm rigid construction) which offers the eco aesthetic with the crush resistance of a rigid box. All designs are ISTA 1A drop-tested before production approval.

Q: What is the minimum order for custom-printed kraft boxes? A: MOQ is 500 units for standard 1–2 color soy-ink printing. Complex multi-color designs (3–4 color CMYK on kraft) typically require 1,000+ units to absorb plate-making costs. We accept trial orders as low as 100 units for unprinted kraft samples.

Q: Can the kraft box be used for food packaging? A: Yes. Our food-grade kraft boxes use FDA 21 CFR 176 compliant paperboard (indirect food contact) and soy-based inks. For direct food contact, we offer additional barrier coatings and liners. Full documentation including FDA and EU 1935/2004 compliance statements is provided with every food-grade order.
