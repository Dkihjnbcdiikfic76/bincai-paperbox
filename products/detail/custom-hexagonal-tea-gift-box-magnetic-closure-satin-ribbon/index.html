FAQ

Q: Is the hexagonal shape more expensive to manufacture than a standard rectangular box? A: The hexagonal shape requires specialized die-cutting tooling and wrap tooling, which adds approximately 10–15% to the tooling cost (one-time). The per-unit production cost is roughly 8–12% higher than an equivalent rectangular box due to the additional hand-assembly steps for the hexagonal wrap. At volumes above 5,000 units, the cost differential narrows to 5–7%.

Q: Can the compartments be customized for different tea types — tea bags, loose leaf pouches, and accessories? A: Yes. We can design a mixed compartment layout: for example, two large compartments for loose-leaf pouches, four narrow slots for tea bags, and one rectangular compartment for a tea spoon or infuser. Provide your product dimensions and we will create a custom die-cut insert mockup within 2 working days.

Q: How does the satin ribbon closure work with the magnetic closure? A: The ribbon serves as both a decorative element and a functional pull tab. When the lid is magnetically closed, the ribbon extends outward for easy gripping. To open, simply pull the ribbon to overcome the magnetic holding force. This eliminates the need for a thumb notch or cutout in the lid, resulting in a cleaner, more premium aesthetic.

Q: What is the lead time for hexagonal boxes vs. rectangular boxes? A: Hexagonal boxes add 2–3 days to the production schedule due to the specialized die-cutting setup and hand-wrapping steps. Typical lead time is 15–18 days for standard orders, 20–25 days for fully custom designs, from artwork approval to FOB shipment.
