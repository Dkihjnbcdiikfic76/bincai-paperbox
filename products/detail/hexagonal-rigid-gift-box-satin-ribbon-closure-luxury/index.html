FAQ

Q: Is the hexagonal shape more expensive than a square box? A: The material cost is comparable — a hexagonal box uses roughly the same board area as a square box of equivalent volume. The main cost difference is die-cutting (hexagonal die tooling ~200 USD vs standard straight-cutting dies) and slightly longer hand-wrapping time (20-30% more labor per box). At 1,000+ MOQ, the per-unit premium is typically 10-15%.

Q: Can the ribbon be replaced with a magnetic closure? A: Yes — we offer a magnetic side-latch alternative where two rare-earth magnets are embedded in the lid and base walls. This eliminates the ribbon entirely for a cleaner look and faster assembly in retail or corporate gift environments (>2,000 units).

Q: What’s the minimum hexagon size you can produce? A: Our Bobst die-cutter handles hexagons down to 80mm point-to-point. Below that, manual hand-wrapping becomes challenging at the corners — we recommend 100mm minimum for production-quality edge wrapping.

Q: Can I have different decoration on each of the 6 faces? A: Yes — our KBA/Heidelberg offset presses print all 6 faces on a single sheet with die-cutting registration marks. Each face can feature different artwork, or you can run a continuous wrap-around pattern. Hot foil stamping is available on any combination of faces.
