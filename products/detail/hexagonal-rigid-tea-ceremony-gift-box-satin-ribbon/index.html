FAQ

Q: Can the hexagonal box be produced with more than six sides — say an octagonal shape? A: Yes. While this listing features the classic hexagonal (six-sided) design, our Bobst SP 102 BMA die-cutter supports custom die shapes including octagonal (eight-sided), round, oval, and custom organic shapes. The same rigid construction, satin ribbon closure, and premium finishing options apply. MOQ for fully custom die shapes starts at 500 units.

Q: Is the satin ribbon removable, or is it permanently attached? A: The satin ribbon is threaded through reinforced die-cut slots in the box base and lid, making it permanently secure but fully functional. It will not detach during shipping or repeated use. For brands that prefer a removable ribbon (e.g., for display-only use), we offer a magnetic hidden closure variant where the ribbon is purely decorative and folds away when not needed.

Q: How many tea bags or loose-leaf tea pouches can each box hold? A: Standard sizes accommodate 6–24 tea bags in individual compartments, or 2–8 pouches of loose-leaf tea (50g–100g each). The internal divider system is fully customizable — we can create custom foam, velvet, or cardboard inserts with die-cut cavities shaped precisely to your product dimensions. Specify your tea bag or pouch dimensions during the sample phase and we will engineer the insert accordingly.

Q: Does the hexagonal shape affect shipping costs compared to standard rectangular boxes? A: Our hexagonal rigid boxes ship flat (knocked down) just like rectangular rigid boxes, reducing shipping volume by 60–75%. When assembled, the hexagonal footprint is approximately 15% larger than an equivalent-volume rectangular box, but we mitigate this through optimized pallet stacking patterns developed over 23 years of export experience. Most clients see a net freight cost increase of less than 8% versus rectangular equivalents.
