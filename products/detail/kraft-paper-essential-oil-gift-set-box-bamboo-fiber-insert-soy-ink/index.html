Quality Assurance

  1. Raw Material Verification: Kraft board grammage ±5%; FSC Chain-of-Custody documentation audit; bamboo fiber tray density and structural integrity; PLA film EN 13432/ASTM D6400 certification verification; soy ink batch certification (ASTM D6868).
  2. Pre-Press: Digital proof on actual kraft substrate — color match ΔE ≤ 2.0; die-line and window alignment verification; ink adhesion test on kraft (3M 610 tape pull — ≤5% removal).
  3. Printing (In-Process): X-Rite eXact spectrophotometer color check every 500 sheets (D65 illuminant); soy ink cure verification — scratch test after 24-hour cure; anti-setoff powder optimized for kraft absorbency.
  4. Die-Cutting & Creasing: Dimensional accuracy ±0.5mm; clean window cut — no fiber tear or hanging chads; crease depth 50% board thickness for clean kraft fold.
  5. Window Patching: PLA film bond ≥4N/15mm (T-peel test); positioning ±0.5mm; 100% visual inspection for wrinkles, bubbles, or adhesive squeeze-out.
  6. Tray Insert QC: Cavity dimensions verified with go/no-go gauge per bottle supplier specs; tray must support 3× bottle weight without deflection; surface smoothness — no rough fibers that could scratch bottle labels.
  7. Assembly & Finishing: Jute twine tensile ≥5kg pull force; tuck flap function — opens/closes 20 cycles without delamination; 100% visual inspection for overall eco-aesthetic consistency.
  8. Finished Goods: ISTA 1A drop test — 10 drops from 760mm, bamboo tray must retain bottles without fracture; compostability verification per ASTM D5338 — ≥90% biodegradation in 180 days; ANSI/ASQ Z1.4 — AQL 2.5 (major), AQL 1.0 (minor).

FAQ

Q: Is the entire box truly compostable, or just certain components?

A: Every component is compostable. The kraft board (FSC-certified, unbleached), bamboo fiber tray (molded without synthetic binders), soy-based ink (ASTM D6868), PLA window film (EN 13432/ASTM D6400 certified), water-based PVA adhesive, and jute twine are all biodegradable. Under industrial composting conditions (58°C ±2°C, controlled humidity), the box achieves ≥90% biodegradation within 180 days per ASTM D5338. The bamboo tray home-composts in 6–12 months. We provide full certification documentation with every shipment.

Q: How does soy-based ink perform on kraft paper compared to standard offset inks?

A: Soy-based inks deliver excellent results on kraft — with one caveat to embrace rather than fight: the natural brown kraft background warms colors slightly (blues become muted, earth tones deepen). This creates a distinctive eco-aesthetic that clean beauty brands actively prefer — it signals “natural” and “artisanal” to consumers. For brands wanting brighter color accuracy, we offer white kraft board (same FSC certification, same compostability) that provides a neutral printing surface. Soy ink coverage, adhesion, and rub resistance are equivalent to conventional inks — we’ve validated this through thousands of production runs on our KBA Rapida 1050 press.

Q: Can the bamboo fiber tray hold heavy 30ml essential oil bottles securely?

A: Yes. Our bamboo fiber trays are engineered for structural integrity — the 2–3mm molded wall thickness, combined with the natural fiber tensile strength of bamboo pulp, supports bottles up to 150g per cavity without deformation. For extra-heavy glass bottles (50ml+), we increase tray wall thickness to 4mm or add a kraft board support platform beneath the tray. We test every new tray design with 3× bottle weight static load for 24 hours to verify zero deformation. The tray’s natural fiber texture also provides grip — bottles don’t slide during transit.

Q: What’s the cost difference between this eco box and a conventional gift box?

A: The cost premium for full compostability is approximately 20–30% vs. a conventional box with petroleum-based inks, PET window, and foam insert. At 1,000 units, a standard 6-bottle kraft eco set box is approximately $3.80–$5.50/unit vs. $3.00–$4.20 for conventional — a $0.80–$1.30 premium. This premium is offset by: (1) the marketing value of “100% compostable” claims — consumers consistently report 30–50% higher purchase intent for verified sustainable packaging; (2) EU plastic packaging tax avoidance (€0.80/kg on non-recycled plastic); (3) brand alignment with clean beauty values that commands premium retail pricing.

Q: Can I get the bamboo tray in a different color to match my brand?

A: Yes. We offer natural pigment coloring for bamboo fiber trays — charcoal grey (plant-based carbon black), terracotta red (mineral iron oxide), and indigo blue (plant-derived). These pigments are biodegradable and food-safe — they don’t compromise compostability. Custom Pantone-matched tray colors are possible but require MOQ 3,000+ units and add approximately $0.40–$0.70/unit. The natural tan bamboo color is the most popular — its warm organic tone contrasts beautifully with amber essential oil bottles.

Q: Does the PLA window fog up or yellow over time like some bioplastics?

A: No. Our PLA film is a high-clarity grade (90%+ light transmission, haze ≤3%) that maintains optical clarity throughout the product shelf life. Unlike early-generation bioplastics that yellowed within months, modern PLA film formulations include UV stabilizers that prevent photodegradation during normal retail display. Accelerated aging tests (40°C / 75% RH for 90 days, equivalent to 18–24 months retail shelf life) show Δ haze ≤2% — imperceptible to consumers. The film remains crystal clear from production to purchase.

Q: What’s the minimum order and can I mix different configurations in one order?

A: MOQ is 500 units per configuration (size + cavity layout). You can mix configurations within the same order — for example, 500 × 3-bottle sets and 500 × 6-bottle sets, totaling 1,000 units. This allows you to offer multiple SKUs without over-committing to any single configuration. Free pre-production samples are provided in 5–7 working days — we recommend sampling both natural brown kraft and white kraft to compare aesthetics. Combined sea freight saves on logistics costs for mixed orders.
