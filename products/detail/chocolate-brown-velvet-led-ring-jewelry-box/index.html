FAQ

Q: How long does the LED battery last, and is it replaceable? A: The standard CR2032 lithium coin cell configuration (2× cells) provides approximately 200 hours of standby time. With an average of 10 lid openings per day at 15 seconds each, this translates to roughly 8–10 months of use before battery replacement. The battery compartment features a tool-free snap-fit cover — customers simply slide the cover open, replace the coin cells, and snap it closed. For high-volume retail display where boxes are opened hundreds of times daily, we offer a rechargeable USB-C LiPo variant (300mAh) that recharges in under 60 minutes and lasts 500+ charge cycles.

Q: Can the velvet color and LED light be customized to match our brand identity? A: Absolutely. We stock over 40 velvet colors and can custom-dye velvet to match any Pantone TCX (Textile Color System) reference — MOQ for custom-dyed velvet is 1,000 units. The LED light is available in warm white (3000K), neutral white (4000K), cool white (6000K), and RGB color-changing with remote control. For brand-specific lighting (e.g., signature blue LEDs, brand signature amber), we can source custom-wavelength LEDs with the same 50,000-hour lifespan — MOQ for custom LED color is 2,000 units.

Q: Does the velvet shed or transfer fibers onto the jewelry over time? A: Our premium velvet undergoes a three-stage finishing process — shearing to uniform 1.0–1.2mm pile height, anti-static treatment to prevent fiber pull-out, and heat-setting at 140°C to lock fibers in place. This results in a shed-resistant velvet that passes ASTM D4966 Martindale abrasion testing at 20,000+ rubs with <1% pile loss. For extremely sensitive jewelry (raw gemstones, high-polish platinum), we recommend our microfiber suede liner option which has zero fiber transfer.

Q: Is the LED module serviceable if it fails after years of use? A: The LED module is designed as a field-replaceable unit (FRU). It connects via a standard JST 2-pin connector and is secured with two Phillips-head screws under the ring slot insert. We provide spare LED modules at $0.40–$0.80 each and can ship replacements within 3 business days. The module is rated for 50,000 hours (5.7 years of continuous operation), so failure during normal use is extremely rare — less than 0.02% field failure rate across all shipments since 2019.
