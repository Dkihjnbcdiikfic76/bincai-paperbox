FAQ

Q: How does the hexagonal shape affect shipping and storage efficiency? A: Hexagonal boxes have approximately 83% of the volume efficiency of rectangular boxes with equivalent dimensions, meaning about 17% more pallet space per unit. However, we mitigate this with custom hexagonal honeycomb master cartons that nest the boxes in alternating orientations, recovering up to 10% of the lost density. For high-volume orders, we can design a collapsible hexagonal variant that ships flat and assembles with a pre-scored locking mechanism — ask our engineering team about this option during design consultation.

Q: How many magnetic closures does a hexagonal box need, and how are they positioned? A: A hexagonal box typically uses two pairs of N35 neodymium magnets (4 magnets total) — one pair on faces 1 and 2 for the lid, and one pair on faces 4 and 5 for the base. For larger boxes (200mm+ flat-to-flat), we add a third pair for additional security. The magnets are counter-sunk flush into the board thickness so the lid sits perfectly flat with no visible magnetic bulge. All magnets are nickel-plated for corrosion resistance and tested to maintain 95%+ pull force after 10,000 open/close cycles.

Q: Can the hexagonal box have different interior configurations for different products? A: Absolutely. Our molded interior options include: single-cavity (one large product), multi-cavity (tea sachets, chocolates), tiered tray (two levels with ribbon lift), and display easel (lid folds back to create a presentation stand). The interior tray can be vacuum-formed PET, flocked EVA foam, die-cut cardboard, or molded pulp — each with its own cost, feel, and sustainability profile. We’ll provide 3D renderings of your interior options at the sampling stage.
