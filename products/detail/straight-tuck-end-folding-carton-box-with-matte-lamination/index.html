FAQ

Q: What is the difference between straight tuck end (STE) and reverse tuck end (RTE) cartons? A: STE cartons have both top and bottom flaps tucking from the same direction (both front-to-back), while RTE cartons have flaps tucking from opposite directions. STE offers a cleaner front panel aesthetic with no flap crease visible. RTE is slightly more material-efficient and commonly used for pharmaceutical cartons. Bincai manufactures both styles.

Q: Can I use food-safe inks and coatings for confectionery or food packaging? A: Yes. Bincai offers FDA indirect food contact-compliant inks, water-based coatings, and low-migration UV varnishes suitable for dry food, confectionery, tea, and supplement packaging. We can also apply grease-resistant internal coatings for oily food products.

Q: What is the minimum order quantity for custom printed folding cartons? A: Standard MOQ is 500 units using existing tooling. For custom structures requiring new die tooling or custom foil stamping plates, MOQ typically starts at 2,000+ units to amortize setup costs. Bincai’s in-house tooling workshop keeps die costs 30–40% below outsourced alternatives.

Q: How quickly can I receive pre-production samples? A: Free physical samples with material swatches, print proofs, and structural samples are provided within 3–5 working days. Rush sampling (48 hours) is available for urgent projects.

Q: Do you support braille embossing for pharmaceutical or accessibility packaging? A: Yes. Bincai provides braille embossing compliant with EU Directive 2001/83/EC and FDA 21 CFR requirements for pharmaceutical OTC packaging. Multi-level embossing dies are produced in-house.
