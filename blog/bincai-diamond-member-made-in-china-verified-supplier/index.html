What To Look For When Evaluating MIC Diamond Members

If you’re using MIC to source packaging (or any manufactured product), here’s a practical checklist beyond the Diamond badge:

  1. Read the audit report, not just the badge. MIC provides a detailed verification report. Look for specific equipment names (not “printing machine” but “KBA Rapida 105 4-color”), actual square footage, and inspection photos showing running production lines.

  2. Cross-reference certifications. Ask for the certificate numbers (not just the logos) and verify them on the issuing body’s database. Bincai’s ISO 9001 certificate can be independently verified through SGS or the certifying body.

  3. Request a video tour. A Diamond audit is a snapshot. A live video walkthrough — where you direct the camera — confirms ongoing operations, not staged scenes. Bincai offers this to all serious buyers: point your phone at any part of our factory, and we’ll show you what’s running right now.

  4. Check product specialization. A Diamond Member selling everything from socks to solar panels is likely a trading company. Bincai has manufactured paper boxes — and only paper boxes — for 23 years.

  5. Verify shipping records. Diamond Members with export history can provide bill of lading samples. Matching shipping volumes to stated capacity is the ultimate reality check.
