Beyond the Print: Die-Cutting, Foil Stamping & Finishing at Bincai

June 10, 2026

If offset printing puts the artwork on paper, die-cutting and finishing give the box its soul. At Bincai’s 18,000 sqm factory (10,000+8,000 smart factory) in Foshan, where 1.7 million color boxes and 220,000+ rigid gift boxes ship daily, the difference between “good enough” and “luxury” lives in fractions of a millimeter — and in the right equipment, operated the right way.

The Die-Cutting Core: Bobst Flatbed Accuracy

After sheets come off our KBA Rapida 1050 and Heidelberg Speedmaster 7+1 UV presses, they enter the die-cutting department. We run Bobst SPeria flatbed die-cutters — the gold standard in paperboard converting:

ParameterSpecification
Maximum sheet size1050 × 750 mm
Crease depth accuracy±0.05 mm
Cut registration±0.15 mm to print
Die lifeUp to 1 million impressions between re-sharpening
Throughput7,500 sheets/hour per machine

Why Bobst over rotary? Flatbed die-cutting gives us cleaner cuts on heavy greyboard (up to 3.0mm), essential for rigid box trays and magnetic closure bases. Every die is custom-made — laser-cut from 0.71mm or 1.05mm steel rule — specific to your product’s structural design.

Hot Foil Stamping: Heidelberg Cylinder Precision

Nothing communicates “premium” faster than metallic foil on a matte black box. We run Heidelberg cylinder foil stamping machines with:

  • Temperature control to ±2°C — critical for consistent foil release on textured papers
  • Pressure profiling adjustable per-square-centimeter — prevents crushing embossed areas
  • Foil colors: gold, silver, rose gold, copper, holographic, matte metallic, and custom Pantone-matched foils
  • Multi-level emboss + foil — raised tactile lettering with foil overlay in a single pass

Embossing & Debossing: Texture You Can Feel

Blind embossing (no foil, just raised/recessed texture) adds dimension without adding cost. Our embossing dies are magnesium or brass — magnesium for short runs (up to 50K), brass for high-volume campaigns:

  • Single-level emboss: Raised logo or pattern, 0.3–0.8mm relief
  • Multi-level emboss: Sculpted 3D effect with variable depth — used for detailed crests, logos, and artwork
  • Registered emboss: Perfectly aligned to print artwork (±0.2mm)
  • Combination foil + emboss: Metallic shine with tactile depth in one station

Spot UV Coating

A high-gloss liquid coating applied to specific areas — logos, product names, design accents — that cures instantly under UV light on our Heidelberg 7+1. The contrast between a spot-UV gloss logo and a matte-laminated background is one of the most requested finishes in cosmetics and electronics packaging.

Aqueous Coating

Water-based coating applied inline during offset printing on the KBA 1050. Available in gloss, matte, and satin finishes. Food-safe formulations available.

Window Patching: See the Product Inside

For folding cartons that need product visibility — tea boxes, chocolate packaging, toy boxes — we apply die-cut PET or PVC windows with:

  • Adhesive: Hot-melt or water-based, FSC-compliant options
  • Film thickness: 0.2–0.5mm PET (PVC-free available)
  • Anti-fog treatment: For refrigerated or frozen food packaging
  • Registration: Window aligned to print artwork within ±0.5mm

The QC Chain for Finishing

Every finishing process has its own checkpoint:

  1. Foil stamping: Coverage inspection under 10× loupe — edge crispness, fill uniformity, no flaking
  2. Embossing: Depth measured with digital micrometer at 5 points per sheet — tolerance ±0.05mm
  3. Lamination: Peel-strength tested per ASTM D903 — minimum 2.5 N/cm for BOPP film
  4. Spot UV: Registration to print checked on every 500th sheet — misalignment >0.3mm triggers adjustment
  5. Window patching: Pull test on window film — must withstand 15N without delamination
