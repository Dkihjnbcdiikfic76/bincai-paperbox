Corrugated Mailer Box Design Guide: Flute Types, ECT Ratings, and FBA Sizing for E-Commerce

June 11, 2026

The E-Commerce Packaging Workhorse

Corrugated mailer boxes are the backbone of e-commerce fulfillment — protecting products from warehouse to doorstep while doubling as a branded unboxing experience. At Guangdong Bincai Color Printing, we manufacture millions of corrugated mailers annually using Heidelberg 7+1 UV and KBA 1050 4-color offset presses, delivering print quality that turns a shipping container into a marketing asset.

Flute Types: Choosing the Right Structure

Flute profile determines cushioning, stacking strength, and print surface quality. Here’s how to match flute type to your product:

ECT Ratings and Structural Performance

Edge Crush Test (ECT) measures stacking strength — critical for palletized shipping and warehouse storage:

GradeECT RatingTypical Use
23 ECT23 lb/inLight mailers, apparel, single-item shipments
32 ECT32 lb/inStandard e-commerce, cosmetics, books
44 ECT44 lb/inHeavy goods, multi-item sets, international shipping
51 ECT (DW)51 lb/inIndustrial parts, bulk shipments, export pallets

Bincai tests every production batch to TAPPI T-811 standards, with our 32+ ECT standard mailer exceeding most e-commerce requirements.

Amazon FBA Sizing Compliance

Frustration-free packaging isn’t just a nice-to-have — it’s a requirement for Amazon FBA sellers. Key dimensions to hit:

  • Tier 1 (Small Standard): Under 15×12×0.75 inches, under 12 oz
  • Tier 2 (Large Standard): Under 18×14×8 inches, under 20 lb
  • Tier 3 (Small Oversize): Under 60 inches length+girth, under 70 lb
  • Tier 4 (Medium Oversize): Under 108 inches length+girth, under 150 lb

We design mailers to target Tier 1 and Tier 2 for maximum FBA fee efficiency — every millimeter saved on excess void fill translates directly to lower per-unit fulfillment costs.
